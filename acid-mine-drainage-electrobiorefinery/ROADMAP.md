# AMDEB Development Roadmap

> From concept to ubiquity — five phases spanning 2026–2050.

---

## Phase 1: Concept & Feasibility (2026–2028)

**Goal:** Validate the core scientific claims and de-risk the highest-uncertainty components.

Lab-scale demonstration of each stage independently. The bioelectrochemical MFC is tested with real AMD from 3–5 field sites (Appalachia, Iberian Pyrite Belt, Copperbelt) to confirm 0.3–2.0 kWh/m³ power output and schwertmannite REE capture efficiency (70–95%). The lanmodulin (LanM) peptide column is validated for REE selectivity at pH 1–2 with real AMD leachate (target: 10⁹× selectivity over Fe/Al, 500+ cycles). The potential-stepped electrowinning stack is tested with synthetic and real AMD to confirm sequential Cu → Ni/Co → Zn → Mn deposition at >85% recovery per metal. The carbonate aggregate process is validated for compressive strength (20–40 MPa) and CO₂ sequestration (0.1–0.3 t/m³). A system-level mass-energy balance model is built and peer-reviewed. **TRL 2→3.** Budget: $2–5M (national lab + university partners). Key milestone: published proof that the integrated 4-stage process is thermodynamically and kinetically feasible.

## Phase 2: Prototype (2028–2031)

**Goal:** Build and operate the first integrated AMDEB module at a single AMD site.

A single 20-ft container prototype is fabricated and deployed at a high-REE AMD site in West Virginia or Pennsylvania (Appalachian coal country). The prototype processes 10–50 L/min continuously for 12+ months, demonstrating all four stages in series: self-powering MFC, sequential electrowinning, LanM REE recovery, and carbonate aggregate production. Key targets: net positive energy (MFC output > system consumption), REE oxide produced at >95% purity, effluent meeting EPA NPDES limits, and aggregate passing ASTM C33 concrete aggregate specifications. Telemetry and remote control validated over LoRaWAN. Biosafety of LanM vesicles and Desulfovibrio confirmed under field conditions. **TRL 4→5.** Budget: $8–15M (DOE + private capital). Key milestone: 12 months of unattended continuous operation with revenue-grade product outputs.

## Phase 3: Pilot Fleet (2031–2036)

**Goal:** Deploy 10–50 modules across 5–10 diverse AMD sites; prove economics at scale.

Modules are deployed at geographically and chemically diverse sites: Appalachian coal AMD (high REE, moderate metals), Iberian Pyrite Belt (extreme acidity, high Cu/Zn), Congolese Copperbelt (high Cu/Co), and a cold-climate site (seasonal flow variability). Each cluster is operated by a local entity (community cooperative, mining company, or state agency) with remote monitoring from a central operations center. The pilot validates: per-module revenue ($11K–140K/yr), OPEX ($0.02–0.08/m³), payback period (1–4 yr), and effluent quality across site heterogeneity. LanM peptide production is scaled to 100 kg/yr via contract fermentation. Supply chain established for MFC electrodes, membranes, and control electronics. Regulatory pathway cleared: EPA NPDES permits, biosafety approvals, and carbon credit verification methodology established. **TRL 5→6.** Budget: $20–50M. Key milestone: 10+ modules operating profitably for 24+ months with independently audited revenue and environmental metrics.

## Phase 4: Industrial Scale (2036–2042)

**Goal:** Manufacture and deploy 10,000–100,000 modules; establish AMDEB as a standard remediation technology.

Mass production of AMDEB modules at $15,000–30,000 each (100K-unit scale). Deployment across U.S. AML sites (target: 5,000–20,000 modules), European mining districts (Iberian Pyrite Belt, Harz, Massif Central), and selected Global South sites (Copperbelt, Andean mines, Indonesian nickel laterites). A "remediation-as-a-service" business model emerges: operators install modules at no cost to landowners and share revenue. REE oxide from AMD reaches 1,000–5,000 t/yr (5–20% of Western demand). Carbonate aggregate from AMDEB enters construction markets (replacing sand mining in regions with high AMD density). Carbon credits certified under established mineralization methodologies. Standards organizations (ASTM, ISO) publish AMDEB design and performance standards. **TRL 7→8.** Budget: $500M–2B (private + public blended finance). Key milestone: 100,000+ km of streams restored; AMD recognized as a mineral resource, not just a pollutant.

## Phase 5: Ubiquity (2042–2050)

**Goal:** AMDEB deployed at all major AMD sites globally (500,000+); AMD pollution largely eliminated as a global problem.

AMDEB modules are standard infrastructure at every significant mine site — active and abandoned — worldwide. The world's orange rivers run clear. 500,000+ sites treated, 50,000+ km of streams restored, 5,000+ t/yr REE oxide and 50,000–500,000 t/yr base metals recovered from waste. 50–200 Mt CO₂/yr permanently sequestered in carbonate aggregate. The $500B global AMD cleanup liability has been converted into a $50B+/yr resource recovery industry. Environmental mineral recovery is a recognized economic sector employing 500,000+ people globally, with former mining communities as primary beneficiaries. The Rio Tinto — polluted for 2,000 years — runs clear. AMD is no longer a perpetual intergenerational burden but a closed-loop resource cycle. **TRL 9.** The technology is mature, standardized, and self-sustaining — the problem it was designed to solve is largely solved.
