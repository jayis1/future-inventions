# AVDA — Development Roadmap

> From bioacoustic laboratory bench to 500 million people protected — a five-phase path from concept to ubiquity.

---

## Phase I — Concept & Lab Validation (2026–2028)

Establish the bioacoustic evidence base in controlled semi-field enclosures (large netted cages, 5×5×3 m, at partner institutes in Burkina Faso, Kenya, and Brazil). Demonstrate that phase-jittered harmonic jamming waveforms reduce *Anopheles gambiae* and *Aedes aegypti* mating success by >90% compared to silent controls, and that host-seeking maskers reduce landing rates by >50%. Train and validate the edge-AI wingbeat classifier on field-collected audio. Publish 3–5 peer-reviewed papers (vector biology, bioacoustics, IEEE sensors). File core patents on phase-jittered jamming and adaptive species tuning. **Exit criterion:** replicated, peer-reviewed evidence that acoustic mating disruption works at statistically significant scale in semi-field conditions.

## Phase II — Prototype & Field Pilot (2028–2031)

Engineer the first integrated AVDA node prototypes (v0.9 hardware) at ~$120/unit in low volume (500 nodes). Deploy a cluster-randomized controlled trial in 10 villages (5 intervention, 5 control, ~250 households each) in a high-burden malaria district. Primary endpoints: entomological inoculation rate (EIR) reduction at 12 and 24 months; secondary endpoints: clinical malaria incidence, biting rate, mosquito population age structure. Run a parallel *Aedes* urban pilot in a dengue-endemic city (e.g., Yogyakarta or Fortaleza). Collect pollinator biodiversity surveys to confirm zero non-target effect. Engage WHO Vector Control Advisory Group (VCAG) for prequalification pathway scoping. **Exit criterion:** field evidence of >50% clinical incidence reduction and >80% mating disruption with no adverse ecological effects, validated by independent monitoring.

## Phase III — Pilot Manufacturing & Regulatory (2031–2034)

Transfer hardware design to a contract manufacturer (e.g., Jabil, Flex) for pilot production at 100K units/year, driving BOM to <$25/node. Establish QA protocols for acoustic output calibration, solar autonomy, and mesh self-organization. Obtain WHO prequalification or equivalent national regulatory approval (target: Kenya PPB, Brazil ANVISA, India CDSCO as anchor regulators). Run a 50,000-person demonstration deployment across 3 districts with ministries of health, integrating AVDA data dashboards into national health information systems. Develop the community installation and maintenance training curriculum; certify the first 500 field technicians. **Exit criterion:** regulatory approval in ≥2 endemic countries, manufacturing cost <$25/node, demonstrated operational reliability >99% over 12 months.

## Phase IV — Scale & Regional Deployment (2034–2038)

Scale manufacturing to 5–10M units/year across two or three contract manufacturers. Launch national-scale programs in 3–5 high-burden countries (target: Nigeria, DR Congo, Tanzania, India, Brazil) covering 50–100 million people. Integrate AVDA into national vector-control strategic plans alongside LLINs, IRS, and emerging malaria vaccines. Establish regional repair and refurbishment hubs (5-year device life with battery replacement at year 4). Publish population-level impact data: projected 30–60 million infections averted annually. Begin urban *Aedes* programs in 20 dengue-endemic cities. Open-source the edge-AI classifier and mesh protocol to enable local manufacturing and adaptation. **Exit criterion:** 50M+ people protected, demonstrated cost-effectiveness <$7/person/year amortized, proven supply chain and maintenance model.

## Phase V — Ubiquity & Global Integration (2038–2045)

Achieve 500M+ people protected across 40+ countries. AVDA becomes a standard layer in the global vector-control stack — as ordinary as bed nets, as invisible as vaccination campaigns. Mesh-gateway data feeds into a real-time global vector surveillance network (the acoustic analog of weather radar), enabling outbreak early warning for dengue, Zika, and malaria resurgence. Cost falls to <$10/node with second-generation MEMS and integrated silicon. National health budgets shift recurring insecticide spend to one-time AVDA capital investment. The technology extends to new vectors: *Culex* (lymphatic filariasis), *Mansonia* (Brugian filariasis), *Simulium* blackflies (onchocerciasis) — any insect that mates by sound. **End state:** mosquito-borne disease transmission is suppressed below the public-health threshold across the endemic world, without a single gram of insecticide, and the 600,000 annual child deaths from malaria become a historical memory.

---

*Each phase is gated by independent evaluation. Failure to meet an exit criterion triggers redesign, not advancement. The roadmap is deliberately conservative — the biology is proven, the engineering is commodity-grade, but the integration and field validation must be rigorous.*
