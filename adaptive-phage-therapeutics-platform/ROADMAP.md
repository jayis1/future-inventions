# APTP — Development Roadmap

> *From concept to ubiquity: the 15-year path to democratizing phage therapy worldwide.*

---

## Phase 1: Concept & Feasibility (2026–2028)

**Goal**: Validate core technologies; demonstrate cell-free phage synthesis at bench scale.

### Key Milestones

| Milestone | Target Date | Success Criteria | Lead |
|-----------|-------------|------------------|------|
| CFPS for T7 phage demonstrated | Q2 2027 | ≥10⁷ PFU/mL yield in *E. coli* lysate system | Academic partner (MIT synthetic biology lab) |
| Microfluidic plaque assay prototype | Q4 2027 | 1,000-well PDMS chip with luciferase detection; >80% concordance with manual plaque assay | Microfluidics partner (Stanford / Fluidigm) |
| PhageFormer v0.1 trained | Q2 2028 | AUC > 0.75 on held-out phage-host pairs; lysis prediction for top 10 pathogen species | AI team (internal + academic) |
| Inkjet DNA synthesis for 50 kb phage genome | Q4 2028 | Synthesize complete T7 genome (40 kb) with <1/5,000 bp error rate after correction | DNA synthesis partner (Twist / custom) |
| Integrated benchtop demo | Q4 2028 | Sequential demonstration: sample in → pathogen ID → phage discovery → CFPS → phage out (may exceed 48 hours) | Integration team |

### Budget: $8–12M

- CFPS optimization: $3M
- Microfluidic chip development: $2M
- PhageFormer training: $2M
- DNA synthesis integration: $1.5M
- Personnel & facilities: $3.5M

### Key Risks

- CFPS yield may plateau below clinical threshold → mitigate with backup bioreactor pouch design
- PhageFormer may not generalize to unseen phage families → mitigate with larger training datasets

## Phase 2: Prototype & Validation (2029–2031)

**Goal**: Build integrated APTP prototype (v1); achieve ≤48-hour timeline in controlled settings.

### Key Milestones

| Milestone | Target Date | Success Criteria | Lead |
|-----------|-------------|------------------|------|
| APTP v1 prototype assembled | Q2 2029 | All 6 subsystems integrated in ISO Class 7 enclosure; automated workflow on touchscreen | Engineering team |
| CFPS yield ≥10⁸ PFU/mL for T7-like phages | Q4 2029 | Consistent yield across 10 production runs; <10% batch-to-batch variation | CFPS team |
| PhageFormer v1.0 validated | Q2 2030 | AUC > 0.85 on held-out species; validated against 500+ experimental lysis assays | AI team |
| 10K-well plaque screening chip validated | Q4 2030 | >90% concordance with manual plaque assay; cross-contamination <0.1% | Microfluidics team |
| End-to-end demonstration (controlled pathogen) | Q2 2031 | *E. coli* or *K. pneumoniae* challenge: sample in → sterile phage cocktail out in ≤48 hours | Full team |
| Animal model proof-of-concept | Q4 2031 | Phage cocktail produced by APTP clears MDR infection in murine model; >80% survival vs. <30% untreated | Preclinical partner |

### Budget: $25–40M

- Prototype manufacturing: $12M
- CFPS yield optimization: $5M
- PhageFormer validation: $4M
- Animal studies: $3M
- Personnel & facilities: $16M

### Key Risks

- Regulatory uncertainty for personalized biologics → mitigate with early FDA/EMA engagement
- CFPS may not work for large/complex phages (Myoviridae) → mitigate with backup bioreactor amplification

## Phase 3: Clinical Pilot (2032–2034)

**Goal**: Conduct Phase I/II clinical trials; obtain regulatory approval in 2–3 jurisdictions.

### Key Milestones

| Milestone | Target Date | Success Criteria | Lead |
|-----------|-------------|------------------|------|
| IND/IMPD submission (US/EU) | Q1 2032 | Regulatory acceptance of APTP as combination product (device + biologic) | Regulatory affairs |
| Phase I safety trial (healthy volunteers) | Q4 2032 | No serious adverse events; phage cocktail well-tolerated; PK data collected | Clinical partner (academic medical center) |
| Phase II efficacy trial (MDR infections) | Q4 2033 | >70% clinical resolution in MDR *E. coli*, *Klebsiella*, *Pseudomonas* infections | Clinical partner (multi-site, 5 countries) |
| Compassionate-use program launch | Q2 2033 | 50+ compassionate-use cases treated; real-world evidence collected | Clinical partner |
| Regulatory approval (first jurisdiction) | Q4 2034 | Market authorization in EU (EMA) or US (FDA) via adaptive pathway | Regulatory affairs |
| APTP v2 (clinical-grade) manufactured | Q4 2034 | 50 units produced to GMP; deployed in clinical trial sites | Manufacturing partner |

### Budget: $60–100M

- Clinical trials (Phase I/II): $30M
- Regulatory affairs: $10M
- Manufacturing scale-up (v2): $15M
- Compassionate-use program: $5M
- Personnel & facilities: $40M

### Key Risks

- Clinical trial enrollment may be slow (rare infections) → mitigate with adaptive trial design and broad compassionate-use program
- Regulatory pathway may require novel framework → mitigate with early engagement and adaptive licensing
- Manufacturing scale may be costly → mitigate with contract manufacturing (Danaher OEM)

## Phase 4: Scale & Deployment (2035–2038)

**Goal**: Manufacture and deploy 10,000+ APTP units globally; establish phage therapy as standard of care for MDR infections.

### Key Milestones

| Milestone | Target Date | Success Criteria | Lead |
|-----------|-------------|------------------|------|
| Regulatory approval (5+ jurisdictions) | Q4 2035 | US (FDA), EU (EMA), India (CDSCO), Kenya (PPB), Brazil (ANVISA) | Regulatory affairs |
| Manufacturing scale: 10,000 units/year | Q4 2036 | Contract manufacturing at 3 regional sites (US, India, EU) | Manufacturing partner |
| Deployment in 50+ LMIC hospitals | Q2 2037 | APTP units operational in district hospitals across 20+ countries | Deployment team |
| Phage genome database: 1M+ entries | Q4 2037 | Open-source database of phage genomes from APTP units worldwide | Data team |
| Cost target achieved: $100/course | Q2 2038 | Consumable cartridge cost ≤$80 at 3M units/year | Supply chain |
| Phase III clinical trial completion | Q4 2038 | >80% clinical resolution across 1,000+ patients; publication in NEJM/Lancet | Clinical partner |

### Budget: $150–250M

- Manufacturing scale-up: $80M
- Global deployment: $40M
- Phase III clinical trials: $30M
- Training & support: $20M
- Personnel & facilities: $80M

### Key Risks

- Supply chain disruption (reagents, chips) → mitigate with dual sourcing and regional manufacturing
- Resistance to adoption (clinical inertia) → mitigate with training programs, published outcomes, WHO endorsement
- Phage genome data sharing concerns → mitigate with open-source licensing and anonymization

| PhageFormer AUC | 0.75 | 0.85 | 0.92 | 0.97 |
| DNA synthesis speed | 24 hours | 12 hours | 6 hours | 2 hours |
| Total time (sample → therapy) | 72 hours | 48 hours | 24 hours | 12 hours |
| Cost per course | $500 | $100–150 | $50–75 | $25–50 |
| Personnel required | PhD operator | Trained nurse | Trained nurse | Autonomous (AI-only) |
| Phage genome coverage | 100 species | 500 species | 5,000 species | All clinically relevant |
