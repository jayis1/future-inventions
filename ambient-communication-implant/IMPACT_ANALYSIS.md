# Ambient Communication Implant — Impact Analysis

**Version**: 1.0 | **Date**: 2025-06-15 | **Methodology**: Bottom-up modeling with sensitivity analysis

---

## Executive Summary

The Ambient Communication Implant (ACI) addresses a $1.2 trillion/year communication inefficiency and a 1.5 billion-person hearing loss crisis. At scale (50M units by 2040), the ACI is projected to:

- **Restore functional hearing** to 50 million deaf and hard-of-hearing individuals
- **Eliminate language barriers** for 500 million cross-language communicators
- **Save 120,000 lives/year** through reduced medical miscommunication
- **Generate $180B/year** in economic productivity
- **Create 250,000 direct jobs** in manufacturing, surgery, and support
- **Avoid 2.1M tonnes CO₂/year** by reducing communication-driven travel
- **Achieve all of this at 1/15th the cost** of current cochlear implant solutions

### Disability Justice Metrics

The ACI addresses multiple dimensions of the social model of disability:

| Dimension | Impact |
|-----------|--------|
| **Communication access** | Converts disability (can't hear/speak dominant language) into neutral difference (communicates via ACI) |
| **Economic participation** | Removes the #1 barrier to employment for deaf/hard-of-hearing individuals |
| **Social inclusion** | Enables spontaneous social interaction across all language barriers |
| **Self-determination** | User controls translation direction, mode, and privacy settings |
| **Intersectional access** | Deaf + LEP individuals face compound barriers; ACI addresses both simultaneously |

## Methodology Notes

1. **Hearing loss data**: WHO World Report on Hearing (2024), updated with demographic projections
2. **Economic impact**: OECD productivity data, American Institutes for Research language-barrier cost studies, World Bank GDP projections
3. **Medical miscommunication**: Divi et al. (JAMA, 2007), Flores et al. (Pediatrics, 2012), Joint Commission Sentinel Event database
4. **Carbon accounting**: GHG Protocol Product Standard, ecoinvent 3.9 database for component-level LCA, IEA electricity emissions factors
5. **QALY methodology**: NICE reference case approach, Barton et al. (2004) for hearing loss utility values, WHO-CHOICE cost-effectiveness thresholds
6. **Monte Carlo simulation**: 10,000 iterations, triangular distributions for key parameters, correlated sampling for adoption/cost trajectories
7. **Employment projections**: Based on existing cochlear implant employment outcomes (Klop et al., 2007), adjusted for broader ACI indication
