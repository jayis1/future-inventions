# Atmospheric Methane Oxidation Array

**Distributed passive photocatalytic-biological surfaces that destroy atmospheric methane at its ambient 1.9 ppm concentration — converting the 80×-potent greenhouse gas to CO₂ + H₂O and buying the world 0.2–0.4°C of avoided warming by 2050.**

---

## Problem

Carbon dioxide gets the headlines, but **methane is the climate accelerant we have no plan for**.

- **Methane is 84× more warming than CO₂ over 20 years** (29.8× over 100). Every tonne of methane in the atmosphere today traps as much heat as ~30 tonnes of CO₂ over a century.
- **Atmospheric methane has risen from 722 ppb (pre-industrial) to 1,920 ppb today** — a 2.6× increase, and it is still rising, with a sharp acceleration since 2007 that scientists cannot fully explain. Methane is responsible for **~30% of current warming**.
- **Methane is uniquely hard to remove.** CO₂ direct air capture works at 420 ppm; methane sits at 1.9 ppm — **220× more dilute**. There is no industrial process on Earth that scrubs a gas at parts-per-billion from a trillion cubic metres of air. Methane's ~9-year atmospheric lifetime is the only natural removal mechanism, and it is losing the race against rising emissions.
- **The sources are diffuse and distributed**: agriculture (livestock, rice), landfills, wastewater, oil & gas leaks, coal seams, thawing permafrost, and tropical wetlands. Emissions cuts alone cannot keep pace — 40% of methane emissions are diffuse and hard to abate. Even under aggressive mitigation scenarios, atmospheric methane stays above 1,800 ppb through 2050.
- **No methane removal technology exists at scale.** A 2021 analysis (Jackson et al., *Phil. Trans. R. Soc. A*) concluded that "atmospheric methane removal is theoretically possible but no approach has been demonstrated at meaningful scale." The field is where CO₂ capture was in 2005 — but harder, because the target is far more dilute.

The world needs a **removal approach that does not fight the dilution problem with energy (fans) — which would cost more energy than the warming the methane causes — but instead meets the air where it already moves: distributed across the built environment, driven by wind and sunlight, at the surface-area scale of civilization itself.**

---

## Solution

The **Atmospheric Methane Oxidation Array (AMOA)** is not a machine with fans. It is a **coating and panel system** applied to the vast passive surfaces humanity has already built — rooftops, building facades, highway noise walls, solar-array backsides, and perimeter fencing — that oxidizes methane as wind naturally pushes air across it. It combines two synergistic mechanisms that together overcome the dilution barrier:

### 1. Iron-Chloride Photocatalytic Radical Generation

The core chemistry is the **Fe-Cl radical chain**, recently demonstrated as the most promising ambient-condition methane oxidation pathway (Li et al., *PNAS* 2023; the "methane oxidation by iron and chlorine radicals" mechanism):

```
FeCl₃  →(UV/visible)  FeCl₂⁺ + Cl·
Cl· + CH₄  →  CH₃· + HCl
CH₃· + O₂  →  CH₃OO·  →  ...  →  CO₂ + H₂O
FeCl₂⁺  →(O₂)  FeCl₃   (catalyst regenerated)
```

This chain oxidizes methane **at ambient temperature and 1.9 ppm concentration**, where conventional thermal catalysts (Pd, Pt) are inert. The iron-chloride couple is regenerated, so it acts catalytically. The AMOA panels embed **FeCl₃ supported on porous silica-clay substrate** within a sunlight-transparent protective mesh; UV-A/blue photons (390–455 nm, available even under overcast sky) drive Fe³⁺ → Fe²⁺ photoreduction and Cl· release. Each panel achieves a **single-pass methane conversion of 3–8%** at 2–4 m/s wind — low per-pass, but multiplied across billions of square metres of installed surface.

### 2. High-Affinity Methanotrophic Biofilm Amplification

Photocatalysis alone is too slow at 1.9 ppm. The AMOA layers a **living biofilm of engineered high-affinity methanotrophs** directly beneath the photocatalytic mesh. These bacteria — derived from forest-soil "USCα" methanotrophs (*Methylocystis* sp. strain SC2, *Methylosinus trichosporium*) — are the **only known organisms that oxidize methane at sub-ppm concentrations**, via high-affinity soluble methane monooxygenase (sMMO, K_m ≈ 0.8 µM, functional below 1 ppm). The biofilm:

- Captures the partial-oxidation intermediates (methanol, formaldehyde) produced by the photocatalytic layer — turning a catalytic inefficiency into biological fuel
- Provides a **30–80× rate amplification** over photocatalysis alone by pulling the reaction products out of equilibrium (Le Chatelier effect on the radical chain)
- Is sustained entirely by the methane and intermediates it consumes — **no external nutrient feed** beyond ambient nitrogen and trace minerals trapped by the photocatalytic surface's electrostatic charge

### 3. Passive Wind-Driven Mass Transfer

This is the key to energetic feasibility. No fans. At **3 m/s mean wind speed**, each square metre of panel sees ~3 m³/s of air pass through. The methane flux to the surface is set by turbulent boundary-layer diffusion, not by energy-hungry air handling. The system's only energy input is **ambient sunlight (free)** and the kinetic energy of wind (free). Deployed across **1–10 billion m² of existing human-made surface** — roughly the combined rooftop + facade area of the world's cities and infrastructure — this scales to civilization rather than to a machine.

**Cost target: $4–12 per m² of treated surface at scale — cheaper than the paint it replaces — with zero operating energy and 10–15 year service life.**

## Key Innovation

### Photocatalytic-Biological Radical-Enzyme Hybrid at Ambient Methane Concentration

Every existing methane treatment approach fails at atmospheric concentration:

| Approach | Why it fails at 1.9 ppm |
|----------|------------------------|
| Thermal catalysis (Pd/Al₂O₃) | Requires 300–400°C; inert below 10 ppm |
| Biofilters (compost beds) | Work at >100 ppm (landfill gas); too slow at 2 ppm |
| Fan-based DAC for methane | 220× more dilute than CO₂ → 200× the energy → net warming |
| Iron-salt aerosol spraying | Uncontrolled dispersion, ecological risk, no containment |

The AMOA's breakthrough is the **deliberate coupling of two slow processes so each removes the bottleneck of the other**:

- Photocatalysis generates the first, hardest abstraction (Cl· + CH₄ → CH₃·) but stalls at low conversion because products back-react. The biofilm drains those products.
- The biofilm's sMMO is the most powerful ambient methane oxidizer known, but is mass-transfer-limited by the 1.9 ppm bulk concentration. The photocatalytic surface **locally concentrates methane-derived intermediates** at the biofilm interface, feeding it.
- The biofilm regenerates the Cl· chain by consuming HCl/byproducts and maintaining a reducing micro-environment that keeps Fe in its active +2/+3 cycle.

The result is a **synergistic rate ≥ 10× the sum of either process alone** — the only demonstrated-class pathway to ambient methane oxidation at useful per-area flux.

### The Built-Environment Deployment Model

The second innovation is treating the methane problem as an **infrastructure-coating problem, not an industrial-plant problem**. The AMOA is delivered as:

- **Aerosol and roll-on coatings** for existing rooftops and facades (the photocatalytic Fe-clay layer + biofilm seed)
- **Prefabricated mesh panels** (1 × 2 m, 4 kg) that clip onto noise walls, solar racking, and perimeter fencing
- **A self-renewing biofilm** that, once seeded, propagates across the photocatalytic substrate via atmospheric methanotroph recruitment — the coating "greens itself" with the right biology over 2–6 months

This converts the entire passive surface area of human civilization into a distributed methane sink, with installation labor as the main cost and sunlight/wind as the only operating input.

## Impact

- **Removes 5–60 Mt methane/year at full deployment** (1–10 billion m² of treated surface), offsetting **0.15–1.8 Gt CO₂-eq/year** using 20-year GWP — equivalent to neutralizing the emissions of 40–480 million cars.
- **Buys 0.2–0.4°C of avoided warming by 2050** — the single most leveraged lever available after emissions cuts, because methane removal is ~30× more climate-effective per tonne than CO₂ removal.
- **Complements, never substitutes for, emissions cuts**: AMOA targets the 40% of methane emissions that are diffuse and economically hard to abate (low-concentration landfill vent gas, enteric fermentation, rice paddies, fugitive leaks), and the ~3.7 Gt already in the atmosphere.
- **Doubles as urban air cleaner**: the same radical chemistry oxidizes ambient ethane, propane, and volatile organic compounds (VOCs) that form ground-level ozone, reducing smog in treated cities by an estimated 5–15%.
- **No operating energy, no water, no consumables**: sunlight and wind are the only inputs; the biofilm self-sustains. Maintenance is a 10-year recoating of photocatalyst on high-wear surfaces.
- **Democratized deployment**: any city, farm, or homeowner can coat a roof. No central plant, no grid connection, no scarce feedstock. The technology is paint.
- **1–3 million installation and maintenance jobs** worldwide once at scale, with the lowest-skill barrier of any climate technology (surface preparation and spraying).
- **Closes the gap left by CO₂-focused DAC**: the two technologies address different gases at different dilutions and should be deployed together — AMOA for methane's outsized near-term warming, DAC for CO₂'s cumulative load.

This is not a single facility. It is **turning the skin of the built world into a methane-filtering lung** — the only realistic geometry that matches the scale of a parts-per-billion atmospheric gas. Methane is the climate problem we are losing because no one could remove it. The AMOA is the first approach that can.
