# Atmospheric Protein Synthesizer — Impact Analysis

Quantitative projections for global deployment scenarios.

---

## 1. Deployment Scale Scenarios

| Scenario | Units | People fed (protein RDA) | Protein output (Mt/yr) | CapEx ($B) |
|----------|------:|:---:|---:|---:|
| Pilot | 100 | 100K | 0.004 | 0.012 |
| Early scale | 10,000 | 10M | 0.37 | 1.0 |
| Moderate scale | 1,000,000 | 1B | 36.5 | 100 |
| Full scale | 10,000,000 | 10B | 365 | 800 |

*Assumptions: 100 kg/day per unit, 365 days/yr, 50g protein RDA per person-day.*

*Addresses the 800M undernourished and the 2B+ with iron/B12 deficiency.*

## 6. Economic Impact

| Protein produced (kt/yr) | 365 | 36,500 | 365,000 |

| Protein value at $3/kg ($B/yr) | 1.1 | 110 | 1,095 |
| Jobs created (manufacturing + ops) | 20K | 2M | 20M |
| Payback period (at $5/kg sale) | 2–4 yr | 2–3 yr | 1–2 yr |
| Food aid savings ($B/yr, if replacing) | 0.4 | 40 | 400 |

*At 1M units: $183B/yr protein market at $5/kg — comparable to the global beef market ($330B).*
*Food aid savings: ~$1,200/tonne delivered → APS at ~$5/kg ($5,000/t) for protein is more expensive per kg but covers complete nutrition (B12, iron) that food aid doesn't.*

## 9. Sensitivity Analysis

| Variable | Base case | Pessimistic | Optimistic | Impact on $/kg |
|----------|---:|---:|---:|---|
| Electrolyzer cost ($/kW) | 700 | 1,200 | 200 | $4.50 → $5.50 → $3.50 |
| Electricity cost ($/kWh) | 0.08 | 0.15 | 0.03 | $4.50 → $6.00 → $3.20 |
| Biomass productivity (g/L/h) | 30 | 15 | 45 | $4.50 → $6.50 → $3.80 |
| Protein content (% dry) | 70% | 55% | 75% | $4.50 → $5.70 → $4.20 |
| Unit cost ($K) | 100 | 150 | 70 | $4.50 → $5.20 → $4.10 |
| Scale (units) | 1M | 100K | 10M | $4.50 → $6.00 → $3.00 |

*Base case protein cost: ~$4.50/kg. Pessimistic (all bad): ~$9/kg (still < beef). Optimistic (all good): ~$2/kg (cheaper than soy protein isolate).*

|--------|---:|---:|
| Beef | 70 | 1.9M |
| Chicken | 110 | 3.0M |
| Soy | 45 | 1.2M |

*At 10M units, APS could supply 73% of current global protein demand — from air, water, and sunlight, using 15 km² of containers (the area of a small airport).*
