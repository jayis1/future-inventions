# Atmospheric Protein Synthesizer — Roadmap

Five phases from concept to ubiquity, 2026–2045.

---

## Phase 1 — Concept & Lab Validation (2026–2028)

The foundational science is proven in the laboratory. The engineered *Cupriavidus necator* strain is built and validated: B12 biosynthesis cassette (*cobA–cobO*), soy leghemoglobin + heme boost, methionine-rich storage protein overexpression, and chaperone robustness (GroEL/GroES, DnaK). A 5-L membrane-delivered bioreactor prototype demonstrates ≥30 g/L/h biomass productivity with k_La ~0.15 s⁻¹ — a 5–8× improvement over sparged gas fermentation. Synthetic auxotrophy (Δ*ilvA*, Δ*thyA*) and kill-switch (*mazF*) are validated for biocontainment. Nutritional output is characterized: PDCAAS >1.0, B12 5–15 µg/g, heme-iron 8–20 mg/g. Two peer-reviewed publications and a patent filing on membrane-delivered sub-LEL gas fermentation. **Exit criteria:** 30 g/L/h sustained for 72 h, complete amino acid profile confirmed, containment <10⁻⁸ survival.

## Phase 2 — Pilot & Regulatory (2028–2030)

A 100-L pilot bioreactor produces 1 kg/day of food-grade protein. The full process chain is integrated: PEM electrolyzer → DAC → membrane fermentation → centrifuge → thermal kill → extrusion → spray drying. A novel-food regulatory dossier is filed with EFSA (EU) and FDA (US), leveraging Solar Foods' 2024 EU approval as precedent. Open-data safety studies: 90-day rodent feeding trial, allergenicity bioinformatic + serum IgE screening, compositional analysis (amino acids, vitamins, minerals, heavy metals, microbiological). Chef partnerships develop product formulations (flour, textured protein, fortified porridge). First public tasting events. **Exit criteria:** 1 kg/day sustained, regulatory dossier submitted, safety studies complete, product formulations validated.

## Phase 3 — Community Unit Prototype & Field Deployment (2030–2033)

The first full-scale 100 kg/day container prototype is built and tested. Six months of unattended operation with remote telemetry. Three field deployments: a refugee camp (humanitarian), a remote community (food security), and an urban facility (proof-of-business). Manufacturing design for cost reduction: target <$120K/unit at 100-unit production run. PEM electrolyzer and silicone membrane module sourcing secured. Operator training curriculum developed. WHO/UNHCR partnership for humanitarian deployment pipeline. **Exit criteria:** 6-month unattended operation, 3 field sites operational, <$120K/unit BOM validated, novel-food approval received in at least one jurisdiction.

## Phase 4 — Scale & Commercialization (2033–2038)

1,000 units deployed across humanitarian, remote-community, and commercial channels. Unit cost drops below $100K; protein cost drops below $5/kg. Manufacturing partnerships with electrolyzer (Nel, Plug Power), membrane (3M, Pall), and bioreactor (Sartorius, Thermo Fisher) suppliers. Open-source operator manual and training program enables local deployment in 50+ countries. Carbon credits certified (methodology: atmospheric CO₂ → food protein, verified by Verra/Gold Standard). Integration with Phosphorus Recovery Network (Invention 019) and Nitrogen-Fixing Bioreactor (Invention 011) for fully closed-loop mineral supply. Flexible-demand operation: APS units ramp up during renewable energy curtailment, storing surplus as dry protein. **Exit criteria:** 1,000 units deployed, <$100K/unit, <$5/kg protein, profitable without subsidy.

## Phase 5 — Ubiquity & Global Food Transformation (2038–2045)

100,000+ units deployed globally. Protein cost below $3/kg — cheaper than any animal protein and competitive with staple crops on a per-protein basis. 500M+ person-days of protein produced per year. 2M+ hectares of grazing land rewilded. 1.5+ trillion liters of water saved annually. 60+ Mt CO₂-eq/year avoided and removed. The APS becomes recognized infrastructure — included in UN humanitarian response kits, national food security strategies, and climate adaptation plans. The concept of "arable land" as a prerequisite for food security begins to fade. Open-source strain and design iterations enable local manufacturing in developing economies. The first extraterrestrial APS unit deployed on a lunar or Martian base (NASA/ESA partnership). **Exit criteria:** 100K+ units, <$3/kg, recognized in UN/national food security frameworks, food system decoupling from land demonstrated at scale.
