# Atmospheric Water Harvester

## Problem

2.2 billion people lack access to safe drinking water. Desalination is energy-intensive and coastal-only. Groundwater is depleting. Rain patterns are shifting unpredictably due to climate change.

## Solution

A modular, solar-powered device that extracts potable water from ambient humidity — even in arid climates (down to 10% relative humidity). Uses metal-organic framework (MOF) nanomaterials that passively adsorb water molecules at night and release them with gentle solar heating by day.

## Key Innovation

- **MOF-801 / MOF-303 sorbent panels** — harvest 6+ liters/day per m² in 20% RH
- **Passive thermodynamic cycle** — no compressors, no pumps, no energy input beyond sunlight
- **Zero liquid discharge** — 99.9% pure water with no brine waste
- **Stackable modules** — household unit (20L/day) to village scale (10,000L/day)
- **Self-cleaning cycle** — UV-C LED sterilization + MOF regeneration

## Target Cost

$50 per household unit at scale (subsidized distribution model for developing regions)

## Impact

- Eliminates water scarcity for 1+ billion people
- Zero energy grid dependency
- No brine pollution (unlike desalination)
- Decentralized — no pipeline infrastructure needed
- Works anywhere with >10% humidity (covers 95% of inhabited land)
