# Development Roadmap — Autonomous Kelp Forest Cultivator (AKFC)

**Document Version:** 1.0  
**Date:** 2026-06-16  
**Classification:** Open — For Review  

---

## Overview

The AKFC development roadmap spans **9 years** from concept to global deployment, progressing through five phases: Concept Validation → Prototype → Pilot → Scale → Ubiquity. Each phase includes specific milestones, success criteria, budget estimates, and go/no-go decision points.

---

## Phase 1: Concept Validation (TRL 2–3)

**Timeline:** 2026–2027 (18 months)  
**Budget:** $3.5M  
**Team:** 15 researchers + 5 engineers

### Objectives
- Prove the core innovations work in laboratory conditions
- Train the AI site selection model on existing data
- Establish partnerships with kelp restoration organizations

### Milestones

| Milestone | Target Date | Success Criteria | Budget |
|-----------|------------|-------------------|--------|
| MFP-5 bioadhesive lab demonstration | Q3 2026 | >1.5 MPa adhesion on granite underwater, <120s cure | $400K |
| Coralline algae composite anchor fabrication | Q4 2026 | Achieve target form factor, compressive strength >4 MPa, dissolution in 120–180 days | $300K |
| Herbivore deterrent capsule production | Q1 2027 | Microfluidic production at 10,000 capsules/hour, Cu release 0.01 mg/day | $200K |
| Integrated anchor + adhesive system test | Q2 2027 | >85% sporophyte retention in seawater tank at 1 m/s current, 90-day survival >80% | $500K |
| Sporophyte nursery bioreactor prototype | Q1 2027 | 20-chamber rotating drum, LED lighting, temperature control, AI vision monitoring | $600K |
| AI site selection model v1.0 | Q2 2027 | AUC ≥0.80 on held-out test set (global transplant data) | $300K |
| SCU hull design & CFD analysis | Q3 2027 | Verified hydrodynamic performance, biofouling resistance, pressure housing integrity | $400K |
| Partnership establishment | Ongoing | 5+ restoration organizations providing data and field access | $200K |
| IP landscape analysis & patent filing | Q4 2027 | Freedom to operate confirmed; 3+ provisional patents filed | $300K |

### Deliverables
- MFP-5 production protocol at 500 L scale
- Anchor manufacturing process validated
- AI model v1.0 trained and evaluated
- SCU conceptual design review complete
- 5+ partnership MOUs signed
- 3+ provisional patent applications filed

### Go/No-Go Decision Point
**Criterion:** Anchor system achieves >70% retention in tank testing AND MFP-5 adhesion >1.5 MPa.  
**If GO:** Proceed to Phase 2.  
**If NO-GO:** Pivot to alternative adhesive (synthetic catechol polymer) or alternative anchoring (mechanical clamp). Budget additional $500K for pivot.

## Phase 2: Prototype Development (TRL 4–5)

**Timeline:** 2027–2029 (24 months)  
**Budget:** $12M  
**Team:** 30 engineers + 15 scientists + 10 operations

### Objectives
- Build and test the first SCU prototype
- Demonstrate end-to-end nursery-to-deployment in controlled conditions
- Conduct first open-water field trials

### Milestones

| Milestone | Target Date | Success Criteria | Budget |
|-----------|------------|-------------------|--------|
| SCU Prototype Alpha (tethered) | Q4 2027 | Submersible navigation, sensor operation, manipulator arm function in seawater tank | $2M |
| Integrated nursery + anchor + deployment (tank) | Q1 2028 | 100 sporophytes successfully deployed in seawater tank, >80% retention at 30 days | $1.5M |
| MFP-5 production scale-up to 10,000 L | Q2 2028 | >8 g/L yield, <$5/g production cost | $1.5M |
| Anchor production scale-up | Q2 2028 | >100,000 anchors produced, < $0.10 each | $500K |
| SCU Prototype Beta (untethered) | Q3 2028 | Autonomous navigation in Monterey Bay harbor, GPS waypoint following, obstacle avoidance | $2.5M |
| SMP Prototype Alpha | Q4 2028 | Surface platform with solar + wave charging, satellite uplink, SCU docking | $1.5M |
| AI model v2.0 (field-trained) | Q1 2029 | AUC ≥0.85 on 90-day survival prediction using combined lab + field data | $500K |
| First open-water deployment | Q2 2029 | 100 sporophytes planted in Monterey Bay (kelp-present site), >75% 30-day retention | $1M |
| First monitoring cycle | Q3 2029 | SCU returns to planted site, assesses growth, replant failures | $500K |

### Deliverables
- SCU Prototype Beta (fully functional, untethered)
- SMP Prototype Alpha (solar/wave powered, satellite linked)
- 100+ sporophytes planted and monitored in open water
- AI model v2.0 validated on field data
- Regulatory pre-application meetings with NOAA, EPA, California Fish & Wildlife

### Go/No-Go Decision Point
**Criterion:** 100 sporophytes planted in open water with >70% 30-day retention AND SCU demonstrates autonomous navigation and return-to-base.  
**If GO:** Proceed to Phase 3.  
**If NO-GO:** Additional 6 months of prototype iteration. Budget additional $3M.

## Phase 3: Pilot Deployment (TRL 6)

**Timeline:** 2029–2031 (24 months)  
**Budget:** $25M  
**Team:** 50 engineers + 20 scientists + 15 operations + 5 regulatory

### Objectives
- Demonstrate multi-unit swarm operation
- Restore 10 hectares of degraded kelp forest
- Generate 1-year survival and growth data
- Obtain regulatory permits for larger-scale deployment

### Milestones

| Milestone | Target Date | Success Criteria | Budget |
|-----------|------------|-------------------|--------|
| 5-unit swarm trial (Monterey Bay) | Q4 2029 | Coordinated coverage of 5-hectare plot, Voronoi tessellation, acoustic mesh communication | $3M |
| 10-hectare restoration plot established | Q2 2030 | 250,000 sporophytes planted across 10 ha, >80% 30-day retention, >65% 90-day retention | $5M |
| SMP integration (1 SMP + 5 SCUs) | Q1 2030 | Solar/wave charging, satellite uplink, autonomous SCU docking and dispatch | $3M |
| 1-year survival data | Q4 2030 | >50% 1-year survival; measurable canopy development; eDNA confirmation of biodiversity increase | $2M |
| Multi-species deployment | Q2 2031 | *M. pyrifera* + *S. latissima* + *L. digitata* in appropriate habitats | $2M |
| Regulatory permit (NOAA/California) | Q3 2031 | Full permit for 500 ha restoration in California waters | $1M |
| Second pilot site (Tasmania) | Q4 2031 | 5-unit deployment in Tasmania, 10 ha restoration | $4M |
| Third pilot site (Norway) | Q4 2031 | 5-unit deployment in Norway, 10 ha restoration | $4M |

### Deliverables
- 3 pilot sites operational (California, Tasmania, Norway)
- 30+ hectares restored
- 1-year survival data published
- Multi-species deployment validated
- Regulatory permits for scaling in 3 jurisdictions
- 3+ peer-reviewed publications
- Carbon credit methodology developed and verified (Verra/VCS)

### Go/No-Go Decision Point
**Criterion:** >50% 1-year survival across pilot sites AND regulatory permits obtained for at least 1 jurisdiction AND carbon credit methodology validated.  
**If GO:** Proceed to Phase 4.  
**If NO-GO:** Extend pilot 12 months. Budget additional $8M for additional sites and species testing.

## Phase 4: Scale-Up (TRL 7–8)

**Timeline:** 2031–2034 (36 months)  
**Budget:** $75M  
**Team:** 100 engineers + 40 scientists + 30 operations + 10 regulatory + 20 business

### Objectives
- Manufacture 200+ SCUs and 20+ SMPs
- Restore 5,000 hectares across 10+ sites globally
- Achieve commercial unit economics ($500–800/ha)
- Establish carbon credit revenue stream
- Build global partnership network

### Milestones

| Milestone | Target Date | Success Criteria | Budget |
|-----------|------------|-------------------|--------|
| Manufacturing facility established | Q2 2031 | SCU production line at 50 units/month, anchor production at 1M units/month | $10M |
| MFP-5 industrial production | Q3 2031 | 100,000 L fermenter operational, <$0.32/g cost, >10 g/L yield | $5M |
| First commercial fleet (10 SCUs + 1 SMP) | Q4 2031 | Fleet deployed at California commercial site, 500 ha/year capacity | $5M |
| 5 commercial fleets operational | Q2 2032 | California (2), Tasmania (1), Norway (1), Chile (1) — 2,500 ha/year total | $15M |
| 10 commercial fleets operational | Q4 2032 | + Japan (1), South Africa (1), UK (1), Canada (1), Iceland (1), New Zealand (1) | $15M |
| Carbon credit revenue begins | Q3 2032 | First Verra/VCS credits issued, $500K+ annual revenue | $2M |
| 5,000 hectares restored (cumulative) | Q4 2033 | Confirmed survival and canopy development across all sites | — |
| SCU cost reduction to $10,000/unit | Q4 2033 | Manufacturing optimization, volume discounts, design simplification | $5M |
| Indigenous community partnerships | Ongoing | 5+ partnerships for traditional ecological knowledge integration | $3M |
| Open-source data platform launched | Q2 2033 | Real-time kelp forest monitoring data publicly available | $2M |
| Third-party environmental impact assessment | Q4 2033 | Independent verification of positive environmental impact, no negative effects | $3M |
| International regulatory framework | Q4 2033 | Deployment permits in 10+ countries | $5M |

### Deliverables
- 10 commercial fleets operational globally
- 5,000+ hectares restored
- Carbon credit revenue stream established
- SCU unit cost below $10,000
- Open-source monitoring platform
- International deployment framework
- 10+ peer-reviewed publications

### Go/No-Go Decision Point
**Criterion:** Commercial deployment at $500–800/ha AND 5,000 ha restored AND carbon credits generating revenue.  
**If GO:** Proceed to Phase 5 (global scale).  
**If NO-GO:** Troubleshoot cost and scale issues. Budget additional $20M for manufacturing optimization and market development.

## Phase 5: Ubiquity (TRL 9)

**Timeline:** 2034–2040+  
**Budget:** $500M+ (leveraged from carbon credits, government, and private investment)  
**Team:** 500+ across manufacturing, operations, research, policy

### Objectives
- Scale to 1,000+ SCUs restoring 50,000+ hectares/year
- Achieve $300–500/ha restoration cost
- Restore 1M+ hectares cumulative
- Establish kelp forest carbon credits as standard climate instrument
- Make the AKFC a globally recognized restoration technology

### Milestones

| Milestone | Target Date | Success Criteria |
|-----------|------------|-------------------|
| 1,000 SCUs manufactured | 2035 | Global manufacturing network producing 200+ SCUs/month |
| 50,000 ha/year restoration rate | 2036 | 100+ fleets operational across 30+ countries |
| 500,000 ha cumulative restoration | 2037 | Verified by independent monitoring |
| $300/ha restoration cost achieved | 2037 | Through manufacturing optimization and scale |
| 10M tonnes CO₂/year sequestration | 2038 | Verified by Verra/VCS |
| 1M ha cumulative restoration | 2040 | "Million Hectare Milestone" |
| Self-sustaining business model | 2038 | Revenue from carbon credits + kelp harvest + government contracts > operating costs |
| Next-generation SCU (v2.0) | 2036 | 50% cost reduction, 2× nursery capacity, 3× deployment rate |
| Kelp forest monitoring satellite constellation | 2038 | Real-time global kelp forest health monitoring |
| Integration with marine protected areas | 2037 | AKFC deployment standard tool in 50+ MPA management plans |

### Long-Term Vision (2040–2050)

| Milestone | Target Date | Vision |
|-----------|------------|--------|
| 50M ha cumulative restoration | 2045 | 10% of restorable coastline |
| 1B tonnes CO₂/year sequestration | 2050 | 1–2% of global emissions |

## Risk Register & Mitigation Strategy

| Phase | Risk | Impact | Mitigation | Cost Impact |
|-------|------|--------|------------|-------------|
| 1 | MFP-5 yield too low | High | Alternative expression systems (E. coli, cell-free); synthetic catechol polymer fallback | +$500K |
| 2 | SCU prototype failure | High | Iterative design; tethered backup mode; modular components | +$3M |
| 2 | Anchor retention <70% in field | High | Redesign anchor geometry; increase MFP-5 volume; test mechanical clamping | +$1M |
| 3 | Regulatory delay (>12 months) | Medium | Multi-jurisdiction parallel filing; early stakeholder engagement; pilot in permissive jurisdiction | +$2M |
| 3 | 1-year survival <50% | High | Improve site selection; deploy heat-tolerant strains; increase monitoring frequency | +$3M |
| 4 | Manufacturing cost >$15K/unit | Medium | Design for manufacturing; simplify components; increase automation; negotiate volume discounts | +$5M |
| 4 | Carbon credit market collapse | Medium | Diversify revenue (government contracts, kelp harvest, equipment leasing) | +$10M |
| 5 | Ocean warming exceeds species tolerance | High | Heat-tolerant strain development; shift to cooler latitudes; assisted gene flow | +$20M |
| 5 | International regulatory barriers | Medium | Local partnerships; technology transfer; adapted designs for local regulations | +$15M |
