# Biodegradable Computing Substrate

## Problem

50 million tons of e-waste generated annually. Less than 20% recycled. PCBs contain lead, brominated flame retardants, and persist for centuries. The computing industry's material throughput is unsustainable.

## Solution

A fully biodegradable computer built on bacterial cellulose substrate with carbon-nanotube circuit traces, organic thin-film transistors, and a wax-encapsulated biodegradable battery. After its 3-5 year service life, it composts in 6 months leaving zero toxic residue.

## Key Innovation

- **Bacterial cellulose PCB** — grown by Komagataeibacter, 100x stronger than plant cellulose, biodegrades in soil
- **Carbon nanotube traces** — deposited via aerosol jet printing, conductive + biodegradable after functionalization
- **Organic TFT processor** — 500 gates, 8-bit, enough for IoT sensing and basic control
- **Wax-encapsulated Mg battery** — magnesium anode + saltwater electrolyte, sealed in beeswax. Composts cleanly.
- **Mycelium housing** — grown into custom shapes, replaces all plastic casings

## Target Cost

$2 per compute unit at scale (vs $15+ for conventional IoT board)

## Impact

- Eliminates e-waste from disposable IoT (projected 75 billion devices by 2030)
- Zero toxic materials in manufacturing or disposal
- Carbon-negative lifecycle (bacterial cellulose sequesters CO2 during growth)
- Enables truly disposable computing for medical diagnostics, agriculture sensors
- Circular economy: composted units feed soil that grows food
