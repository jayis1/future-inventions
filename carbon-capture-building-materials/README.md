# Carbon Capture Building Materials

## Problem

Concrete production emits 8% of global CO₂. Buildings account for 39% of energy-related carbon emissions. We need to not just stop emitting — we need to actively remove CO₂ from the atmosphere while we build.

## Solution

Construction materials (concrete, bricks, insulation, panels) manufactured via carbon mineralization — CO₂ from air or industrial sources is reacted with calcium/magnesium silicates to form stable carbonates that become the structural material. Every brick is a permanently sequestered chunk of CO₂.

## Key Innovation

- **CO₂-mineralized concrete** — replaces Portland cement with CO₂-cured calcium carbonate. Stronger than traditional concrete, carbon-negative.
- **Biochar insulation panels** — pyrolyzed biomass locked into mycelium-bonded panels. R-value 4.5/inch, sequesters 2 kg CO₂ per panel.
- **Atmospheric CO₂ direct capture reactors** — co-located with batching plants, capture 500 tons CO₂/year per unit
- **Magnesium carbonate bricks** — 1 kg CO₂ permanently locked per brick, fired at 200°C (vs 1200°C for clay)
- **Full lifecycle carbon accounting** — every material comes with certified negative-grams-CO₂ badge

## Target Cost

Parity with traditional materials within 3 years of scale-up

## Impact

- Every new building becomes a carbon sink instead of a carbon source
- Eliminates 8% of global CO₂ emissions from cement industry
- Reverses cumulative emissions: a skyscraper built with these materials sequesters ~10,000 tons CO₂
- Compatible with existing construction equipment and methods
- Creates economic incentive to build carbon-negative
