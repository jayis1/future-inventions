# IMPACT ANALYSIS — Electrochemical Blackwater Refinery (EBR)

Quantitative projections for the Electrochemical Blackwater Refinery at three deployment scales. All figures are design estimates grounded in validated prior art and epidemiological models.

---

## Deployment scales analyzed

| Scale | Units deployed | People reached | Timeframe |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pilot | 50 | 300–500 | Years 4–6 |
| Early scale | 1,000,000 | 6–10 million | Years 6–10 |
| Global ubiquity | 100,000,000 | 600 million–1 billion | Years 10–20 |
| Universal | 2,000,000,000 | 3.6 billion | 2050 horizon |

---

## 1. Public health impact

### Diarrheal disease reduction
- **Baseline**: ~500,000 child deaths/yr from diarrheal disease; ~1.7 billion episodes/yr globally (WHO).
- **Causal path**: EBR eliminates the fecal-oral contamination pathway at the source — the most robust intervention point in the sanitation chain.
- **Effect size** (WHO WASH studies): safely managed sanitation reduces diarrheal disease by **28–58%** in target populations.

| Scale | Child deaths averted/yr | Diarrheal episodes averted/yr |
|---|---|---|
| 1M units | 500–1,500 | 5–10 million |
| 100M units | 50,000–150,000 | 1–2 billion |
| 2B units | 200,000–400,000 | 5–10 billion |

### Pathogen elimination at source
- ≥6-log kill of *E. coli*, *Vibrio cholerae*, rotavirus, *Cryptosporidium* oocysts, *Ascaris* eggs.
- No untreated effluent ever discharged (ORP-terminated fail-safe).
- Eliminates groundwater contamination from pit/septic leakage — the dominant drinking-water contamination pathway in LMICs.

### Antimicrobial resistance reduction
- EAOP destroys antibiotic-resistance gene (ARG) fragments (>70% via biochar polishing).
- Breaks the human waste → environment → resistance propagation chain.

## 2. Nutrient recovery & food security

### Phosphorus loop closure
- **Baseline**: Global mined phosphate rock ~45 Mt P₂O₅/yr; human excreta contains ~3 Mt P/yr (~15–20% of mined demand).
- **EBR recovery**: ≥85% of influent P as struvite = 2.5–5.5 kg P₂O₅-equivalent/household/yr.

| Scale | Struvite produced/yr | Phosphate rock replaced | Value (at $500/t struvite) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1M units | 3,000–6,000 t | 0.01–0.02% global | $1.5–3M |
| 100M units | 300,000–600,000 t | 1–2% global | $150–300M |
| 2B units | 6–12 Mt | 20–40% global | $3–6B |

### Nitrogen fertilizer recovery
- 30–60 kg N/household/yr retained in pathogen-free liquid fertilizer.
- At 100M units: 3–6 Mt N/yr — equivalent to ~3–6% of global synthetic N fertilizer (Haber-Bosch, ~120 Mt N/yr), displacing ~30–60 TWh of energy-intensive ammonia synthesis.

### Smallholder farmer benefit
- 500 million smallholder farms in the Global South spend $50–200/yr on synthetic fertilizer.
- EBR outputs ($40–90/yr value) make households **net fertilizer producers** — revenue-positive within 2–4 years.

## 3. Water savings

### Flush water eliminated
- Conventional flush toilet: 30–200 L/person/day (varies by region/cistern).
- EBR: ≤0.2 L/cycle (rinse only, graywater-acceptable).

| Scale | Flush water saved/yr | Equivalent (people-year of water) |
|---|---|---|
| 1M units (6–10M people) | 0.1–0.7 billion m³ | 7,000–50,000 person-years |
| 100M units (600M–1B people) | 10–70 billion m³ | 700,000–5M person-years |
| 2B units (3.6B people) | 40–260 billion m³ | 3–18M person-years |

- Critical in water-stressed regions: 17 countries face "extremely high" water stress (WRI); 25 face "high."

## 4. Climate impact

### Methane avoidance
- On-site sanitation (pit/septic) emits an estimated **30–80 Mt CH₄/yr** globally (≈1–3 Gt CO₂-eq, 100-yr GWP).
- EBR mineralizes organics aerobically via EAOP → **near-zero methane**.

| Scale | CH₄ avoided/yr | CO₂-eq avoided/yr |
|---|---|---|
| 100M units | 3–8 Mt CH₄ | 100–270 Mt CO₂-eq |
| 2B units | 30–80 Mt CH₄ | 1–3 Gt CO₂-eq |

### Carbon sequestration via biochar
- Each biochar cartridge locks ~5–15 kg CO₂ when returned to soil.
- Cartridge replacement: 1–2/year per unit.

| Scale | Biochar CO₂ sequestered/yr | Cartridges/year |
|---|---|---|
| 100M units | 5–30 Mt CO₂ | 100–200M cartridges |
| 2B units | 100–600 Mt CO₂ | 2–4B cartridges |

### Avoided Haber-Bosch energy (via N recovery)
- Synthetic N production: ~1–2% global energy, ~1.2% global CO₂ emissions (~420 Mt CO₂/yr).
- At 100M units: 3–6 Mt N recovered → avoids ~10–25 Mt CO₂/yr from ammonia synthesis.

### Total climate impact at 100M units
- 100–270 Mt CO₂-eq (methane avoidance)
- 5–30 Mt CO₂ (biochar sequestration)
- 10–25 Mt CO₂ (avoided ammonia synthesis)
- **Total: ~115–325 Mt CO₂-eq/yr** — equivalent to taking 25–70 million cars off the road.

## 5. Economic impact

### Household economics
| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Unit CapEx | $180–350 |
| Annual OpEx | ~$0 (biochar $2/yr, locally sourced) |
| Amortized cost/person/yr (10-yr life) | $3–7 |
| Fertilizer value recovered/household/yr | $40–90 |
| Payback period (if fertilizer used/sold) | 2–4 years |
| Net annual benefit after year 4 | $30–80/household/yr (net positive) |

### Macroeconomic impact at 100M units
| Category | Annual value |
|---|---|
| Healthcare savings (averted diarrhea treatment) | $2–8 billion |
| Productivity gains (healthy days) | $5–15 billion |
| Fertilizer value produced | $150–300 million |
| Carbon credit value (at $30/t CO₂-eq) | $3.5–10 billion |
| Flush water value (at $0.50/m³) | $5–35 billion |
| **Total annual economic value** | **$15–70 billion** |

### Cost comparison: EBR vs. conventional sewerage
- Sewerage in LMICs: $2,000–10,000/household CapEx + $20–80/person/yr OpEx.
- EBR: $180–350 CapEx + $3–7/person/yr OpEx.
- **CapEx ratio: 10–30× cheaper. OpEx ratio: 5–10× cheaper.**
- At 100M households: EBR costs $18–35 billion vs. $200B–$1T for equivalent sewerage.

## 6. Dignity, equity & safety

- **493 million people** practice open defecation — EBR provides safe, private, in-home sanitation.
- Women and girls bear the brunt of unsafe shared facilities: assault risk, missed school during menstruation, shame.
- EBR's sealed, odorless, in-dwelling design restores dignity and safety — the most cited non-health benefit in sanitation interventions.
- No literacy required (3-LED + 1-button interface) — universally accessible.

---

## 7. Resilience & disaster risk reduction

- **Flood-safe**: sealed unit, no overflow — critical in flood-prone deltas (Bangladesh, Vietnam, Nigeria).
- **Grid-independent**: operates through blackouts, conflict, and infrastructure collapse.
- **Deployable in <48 hours**: community-cluster units for refugee camps and disaster zones.
- **Cholera containment**: ≥6-log *V. cholerae* kill at source prevents outbreak amplification in displaced populations.

---

## 8. Limitations of this analysis

- Effect sizes extrapolated from WHO WASH meta-analyses; real-world impact depends on adoption rate, maintenance adherence, and local context.
- Pathogen kill validated at bench scale; field validation at TRL 5–7 pending.
- Climate figures use 100-yr GWP (CH₄ = 28× CO₂); 20-yr GWP would be ~3× higher.
- Fertilizer value assumes local market access; in remote areas, value may be subsistence-only.
- Carbon credit pathway for biochar sequestration requires verification framework (Verra/PCO standards evolving).

---

*This analysis is a design-stage projection. It should be refined after Phase 3 (Pilot) field data is available. The orders of magnitude, however, are robust: EBR's impact potential is in the same class as vaccine campaigns and clean water access — the fundamental public health interventions of the last century.*
