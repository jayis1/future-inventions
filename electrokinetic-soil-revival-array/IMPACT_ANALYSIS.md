# ESRA — Impact Analysis

> Quantitative projections for people, planet, and economy at scale (100M ha by 2045).

---

## 1. People & Food Security

| Metric | Value | Basis |
|--------|-------|-------|
| People in salinity-affected regions | 1.5+ billion | FAO 2021 |
| Hectares reclaimable by ESRA | 100–500 million | Of 1.5B ha salt-affected, ~10–30% is cropland treatable in root-zone mode |
| Hectares targeted by 2045 | 100 million | Phase 5 goal |
| Restored crop yield (per ha) | 2–5 t/ha/yr grain equivalent | Salt-degraded land → productive; baseline 0–1 t/ha degraded |
| Additional food produced | 200–500 million t/yr | 100M ha × 2–5 t/ha |
| People regaining food security | 200–500 million | Assuming 0.5 t grain/person/yr consumption |
| Rural jobs created | 1–2 million | Electrode manufacturing, deployment, salt-recovery co-ops, maintenance |
| Migration avoided | 10–50 million people | Coastal delta + Aral basin + Sahel displacement projections |

## 2. Planet — Water, Carbon, Land

| Metric | Value | Basis |
|--------|-------|-------|
| Fresh water saved vs. leaching | 50–150 Gt over 20 yr | 100M ha × (5,000–15,000 − 2) m³/ha; conservative mid-range |
| Water saved per year (peak) | 2.5–7.5 Gt/yr | Distributed over treatment cycles |
| Carbon sequestered (biochar) | 2–8 t CO₂/ha/yr | Stable aromatic carbon, centuries-scale permanence |
| Total carbon over 20 yr (100M ha) | 200–800 million t CO₂ | Lower bound conservative; biochar C content ~70% |
| Forest/savanna spared from conversion | 30–50 million ha | Reclaiming 100M ha cropland avoids clearing ~0.3–0.5 ha new land per ha reclaimed |
| Salt recovered as product | 300–800 million t/yr (at scale) | 3–8 t/ha × 100M ha; gypsum + NaCl + epsomite |
| Industrial NaCl mining displaced | 10–30% of global demand | ~280 Mt/yr global NaCl market; ESRA yields 100–500 Mt NaCl fraction |

## 3. Economics

| Metric | Value | Basis |
|--------|-------|-------|
| Treatment cost per ha (at scale) | $500–1,250 | CapEx $680–1,200 + O&M; falls to <$500 at 1M-unit scale |
| Restored land value (production) | $500–2,000/ha/yr | Crop output on reclaimed land |
| Payback period | 1–2 crop cycles | Treatment cost / annual restored production |
| Net benefit per ha over 10 yr | $3,000–18,000 | (Restored production − treatment − maintenance) × 10 yr |
| Net benefit at 100M ha (10 yr) | $300 billion – $1.8 trillion | Cumulative; dominates treatment cost by 10–50× |
| Salt-recovery revenue | $30–120/ha/cycle | 3–8 t × $10–15/t mixed salt market value |
| Carbon credit revenue | $30–120/ha/yr | 2–8 t CO₂ × $15/t voluntary market price |
| Total co-product revenue (10 yr) | $60–240/ha/yr | Salt + carbon credits offset 10–40% of treatment cost |

## 4. Equity & Resilience

| Metric | Value | Notes |
|--------|-------|-------|
| Deployable without grid power | ✅ | Solar-only operation |
| Deployable without heavy machinery | ✅ | Two workers, hand auger |
| Open-source hardware & inoculants | ✅ | MIT license; community bio-bank network |
| Unit ownership model | Cooperative | Farmer co-ops own, operate, and profit from salt recovery |
| Dependency on agrochemical supply chains | Eliminated | Microbial inoculants are freeze-dried, shelf-stable, locally producible |
| Coastal delta defense tool | ✅ | Counters sea-level-driven saltwater intrusion — a climate adaptation measure |

## 5. Scenario Comparison — 100M ha by 2045

| Path | Water used | Cost | Carbon | Land outcome |
|------|-----------|------|--------|--------------|
| **ESRA** | 0.1–0.2 Gt | $50–125B | −200 to −800 Mt CO₂ (sequestered) | 100M ha restored, productive |
| Conventional leaching | 500–1,500 Gt (unavailable) | $500B–1.5T | 0 | Infeasible in arid regions |
| Subsurface drainage | 200–800 Gt | $200B–600B | 0 | Partial, slow (1–3 yr/ha) |
| Do nothing / land retirement | 0 | $0 | 0 | 100M ha lost; 30–50M ha new land cleared |

**Bottom line:** ESRA is the only path that is simultaneously water-feasible, cost-effective, carbon-negative, and community-deployable at the scale the problem demands. The binding constraint was never technology — it was fresh water. ESRA removes that constraint.
