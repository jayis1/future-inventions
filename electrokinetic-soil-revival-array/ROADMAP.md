# ESRA — Development Roadmap

> From bench-scale concept to 100 million hectares of restored farmland by 2045.

---

## Phase 1 — Concept & Lab Validation (2026–2028)

Bench-scale electrokinetic columns (30 cm Ø × 60 cm) packed with synthetic saline soils (ECₑ 4–16 dS/m) validate ion-transport rates, electrode resistivity targets, and polarity-reversal protocols. Concurrent mesocosm experiments test the halotolerant microbial consortium under bioelectric fields, measuring EET enhancement, hyphal extension, and community shifts via 16S/ITS sequencing. Electrode formulation is iterated across biochar feedstocks (rice husk, coconut shell, cotton stalk) to hit <0.5 Ω·m bulk resistivity with 3–7 yr degradation profile. Output: TRL 3→4, peer-reviewed validation of the three-mechanism integration, optimized electrode recipe, and a field-ready controller prototype.

## Phase 2 — Prototype & Field Pilot (2028–2030)

First 1-ha and 10-ha field deployments in three contrasting sites: Indus Delta (coastal salinity), Aral basin (legacy irrigation), and Murray–Darling (dryland salinity). Each pilot runs a full 90–180-day treatment cycle with instrumentation: multi-depth EC probes, brine yield tracking, microbial community sampling, and crop-establishment trials post-treatment. Goal: demonstrate 80%+ root-zone salt removal, <2 m³/ha water use, and successful crop re-establishment within 1–2 cycles. Salt-recovery yield and quality are validated against market specifications. Output: TRL 5→6, field-validated performance numbers, deployment playbook.

## Phase 3 — Commercial Pilot & Manufacturing (2030–2032)

First commercial ESRA kits produced at $1,200/ha via manufacturing partnerships: biochar electrodes from regional ag-waste pyrolyzers, controllers and PV from electronics OEMs, microbial inoculants from biotech partners. Regulatory approval secured in 3 lead countries (Pakistan, Uzbekistan, Australia). 1,000 units deployed to early-adopter cooperatives with performance-based financing (pay-on-restored-yield). Open-source hardware schematics and inoculant recipes published. Output: TRL 7, validated unit economics, trained deployment network, regulatory pathway.

## Phase 4 — Scale Deployment (2032–2040)

1 million hectares treated across 15+ countries. Unit cost falls below $500/ha as electrode manufacturing scales and PV/salt-battery components commoditize. Open-source microbial inoculant production network (community bio-banks) eliminates supply-chain dependency. Carbon-sequestration credits (2–8 t CO₂/ha/yr biochar) monetized via voluntary carbon markets, subsidizing treatment for low-income farmers. Integration with salt-tolerant crop breeding programs and modernized drip irrigation locks in post-treatment productivity. Output: TRL 9, self-sustaining deployment ecosystem, proven carbon-negative remediation.

## Phase 5 — Global Ubiquity (2040–2045)

100 million hectares restored — an area larger than Egypt — returning to productive agriculture. ESRA becomes standard practice for arid-zone soil remediation, integrated into national land-restoration policies and FAO programs. Salt-recovery cooperatives supply regional gypsum, NaCl, and epsomite markets; surplus reverse-ED batteries form village microgrids. The technology is embedded in climate-adaptation infrastructure for coastal deltas facing sea-level-driven saltwater intrusion. By 2045, soil salinization is no longer a one-way ratchet: it is a reversible, carbon-negative, community-managed process. The land that was lost comes back.
