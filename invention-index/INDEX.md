- **Impact Potential:** 1.6B+ people without reliable refrigeration; 200–400 Mt/year food saved from post-harvest spoilage (feeding 500M–1B people); 50M+ vaccine doses/year saved from cold chain failure; 20,000–50,000 lives/year saved from improved vaccine/medicine access; 50–100 Mt CO₂/yr avoided from HFC + fossil fuel displacement plus 0.5–1.5 Gt CO₂-eq/yr from reduced food waste; 500M+ smallholder farmers gaining 20–60% income from perishable storage
- **Key Material:** CaCl₂-impregnated activated biochar (rice husk/coconut shell) adsorbent with 0.5–0.6 g/g water uptake, desorbing at 60–85°C from flat-plate solar thermal collector; SiO₂/PDMS or BaSO₄ radiative sky cooling metamaterial coating (solar reflectivity >0.96, thermal emissivity >0.95 @ 8–13 µm atmospheric window) on condenser; vacuum insulation panels (λ <0.004 W/m·K); water as refrigerant + ice phase-change battery (334 kJ/kg)
- **Key Innovation:** First coupling of three free environmental thermal resources into one continuous refrigeration cycle — solar heat drives desorption (day), radiative sky cooling lowers condenser temperature 5–15°C below ambient (day + night, improving COP from 0.20–0.27 to 0.34–0.53 — a 1.5–2.5× improvement over air-cooled adsorption), and nighttime ambient cooling drives adsorption (night). CaCl₂-biochar composite achieves 2.5–5× higher water uptake than silica gel at 60–85°C desorption (compatible with cheap flat-plate collectors, no tracking, no vacuum tubes). Ice phase-change battery bridges day/night intermittency — 10–15 kg ice provides 24–48 h holdover. Zero electricity, zero fuel, zero moving parts, zero refrigerant GWP. $80–215 vs. $2,000–7,000 for solar-PV compressor refrigerators.
