# Lithium Brine Bioscavenger — Quantitative Impact Analysis

**Document version:** 1.0
**Date:** 2026-06-18
**Analysis basis:** Full-scale deployment of 1,000,000 LBB modules by 2045

---

## Executive Summary

The Lithium Brine Bioscavenger (LBB) at full scale (1M modules by 2045) would produce **500,000–1,000,000 tonnes of battery-grade Li₂CO₃ per year** from currently-stranded dilute brines — representing **25–50% of projected 2050 global lithium demand**. This analysis quantifies the environmental, social, and economic impacts across water, land, carbon, ecosystems, jobs, geopolitics, and the energy transition.

## 2. Environmental Impact

### 2.1 Water Savings

| Metric | Evaporation Pond | LBB | Savings |
|---|---|---|---|
| Water per kg Li₂CO₃ (L) | 500,000 | 5–10 | 499,990–499,995 |
| Annual water use at 1M t/yr (billion L) | 500 | 5–10 | **490–495 billion L/yr saved** |

| Lithium Triangle aquifer preservation | Depleted | Preserved | Salar de Atacama, Uyuni, Hombre Muerto ecosystems protected |

**Context:** The Salar de Atacama receives <15 mm rainfall/year. Evaporation ponds extract 2,000+ L/s of brine, depleting the aquifer and causing subsidence. At 1M t/yr LBB deployment replacing equivalent evaporation capacity: **490 billion L/yr of brine stays in the aquifer** — preventing water table decline and wetland loss.

| Metric | Value |

## 8. Long-Term Vision (2050+)

By 2050, the LBB network produces 500K–1M t Li₂CO₃/yr from dilute brines and seawater. Beyond 2050:

| Horizon | Capability | Impact |
|---|---|---|
| 2050–2055 | Seawater FO-LBB at scale (100K+ modules) | Unlimited Li supply — every coastline is a Li resource |
| 2050–2060 | Multi-mineral LBB (Li + Sr + REEs + Mn) | Single module extracts 3–5 critical minerals from one brine |
| 2055–2065 | In-situ subsurface LBB (permeable reactive barriers) | Zero surface footprint — Li extracted in reinjection wells |
| 2060–2070 | Solid-state electrolyte direct synthesis | LBB produces LLZO garnet directly — eliminates 3–4 downstream stages |
| 2070+ | Bio-extractive mining platform (beyond Li) | Generalized platform for biological extraction of any carbonate-precipitating mineral from dilute solutions |

**The ultimate vision:** By 2070, biological extraction replaces chemical extraction as the default mining modality for all dilute/mineralized brine resources — not just lithium, but strontium, rare earths, manganese, and any metal whose carbonate has low K_sp. The LBB is the first proof of concept for a **bio-extractive mining paradigm** that is solar-powered, distributed, community-owned, and regenerative by design.
