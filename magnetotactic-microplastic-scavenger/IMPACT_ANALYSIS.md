## 7. Key Assumptions & Uncertainties

- **Regulatory approval** by 2031 (US/EU) — if delayed 5 years, all timelines shift +5 yr
- **Public acceptance** of engineered bacteria in WWTPs — critical; mitigated by wastewater-only deployment
- **Hydrophobin binding on aged/biofilm-coated MPs** — 10–15% lower efficiency assumed; may require pre-treatment
- **Magnetic capture at high flow** — validated to 10 m/h in lab; field validation at 500 m³/day is Phase 3 milestone
- **Solar-Polymer Upcycler co-location** — not required for MMS to function, but required for full circular loop
- **Microplastic health effects** — still uncertain; this analysis assumes precautionary principle (removal is beneficial regardless)

## Conclusion

The MMS is a **leapfrog technology**: it does not incrementally improve membrane filtration — it replaces the paradigm with a self-renewing biological agent and a zero-energy magnetic filter. At full scale (2050), it can remove more microplastic from water than all current technologies combined, at 1/100th the cost, while feeding a circular plastic economy. The primary risks are regulatory and social, not technical — the science is grounded in well-characterized magnetotactic bacteria, well-understood hydrophobin surface chemistry, and standard magnetic separation physics. The path from lab to ubiquity is long (25 years) but every phase has clear deliverables, clear funding sources, and clear regulatory milestones.
