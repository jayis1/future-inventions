# Magnetotactic Microplastic Scavenger — Roadmap

**From concept to ubiquity: a 25-year deployment plan to close the microplastic loop.**

---

## Phase 1: Concept (2026–2028)

Validate the three core scientific claims in academic labs: (1) hydrophobin surface display on *M. magneticum* AMB-1 via InaZ anchor achieves universal plastic binding (K_D < 10⁻⁷ M) across PE, PP, PS, PET, PVC, PA, PU; (2) *mamAB/feoAB* overexpression doubles cellular magnetic moment (verified by SQUID magnetometry); (3) magnetic separation of cell-plastic complexes in a benchtop Halbach column achieves >80% capture at 1–10 m/h. Deliverables: 3+ peer-reviewed papers, engineered strain deposited in a type culture collection, provisional patents filed on the hydrophobin-magnetosome fusion construct and the Halbach separation geometry. Budget: $2–4M (academic grants + seed).

## Phase 2: Prototype (2028–2031)

Scale from benchtop to a 1–5 m³/day pilot unit. Optimize the fluidized-bed contact reactor (glass bead size, bed expansion, residence time), validate binding kinetics in real secondary effluent (not just synthetic), and integrate the regrowth chemostat. Engineer the three-layer biocontainment (Δ*dapA* auxotrophy, *mazF* kill switch) and prove <0.01% environmental survival in 7-day mesocosm tests. Build the control system (STM32 + sensor suite + LoRa telemetry). First regulatory engagement: EPA TSCA pre-submission consultation, EU contained-use notification. Deliverable: a working prototype that treats 1 m³/day of real WWTP effluent at >85% MP removal. Budget: $8–15M (Series A + government demos).

## Phase 3: Pilot (2031–2035)

Deploy 5–10 field pilots at partner WWTPs (100–500 m³/day each) in 3 countries (US, EU, Singapore). Validate 6-month continuous operation: capture efficiency, energy consumption, biocontainment integrity, recovered-plastic quality. Co-locate 2 pilots with Solar-Polymer Upcycler prototypes to demonstrate the closed-loop monomer pathway. Launch the household washing-machine cartridge field trial (1,000 homes). Win first regulatory approvals (TSCA, EU). Deliverable: certified 500 m³/day module with validated LCA and safety dossier. Budget: $30–60M (Series B + pilot grants).

## Phase 4: Scale (2035–2040)

Commercial manufacturing of WWTP modules (target: 10,000 units/year) and household cartridges (target: 100M units/year). Secure EU Ecodesign Directive mandate for microfiber filters. Retrofit 500–1,000 WWTPs globally. Build centralized regrowth-culture supply chain (regional biomanufacturing hubs producing freeze-dried starter cultures). Drive module cost below $8,000. Train 10,000+ installation/maintenance technicians. Begin deployments in developing-world cities via Green Climate Fund and World Bank water programs. Deliverable: 50,000+ WWTP modules + 500M+ household filters deployed; 0.5–1 Mt/year microplastic captured. Budget: $500M–1B (Series C / IPO / sovereign green bonds).

## Phase 5: Ubiquity (2040–2050)

Universal deployment: every major WWTP (>100,000 plants) retrofitted; every new washing machine MMS-equipped by default; rural drinking-water units in 50+ countries. Microplastic riverine input to oceans reduced 90%+. Recovered-plastic feedstock feeds a global network of Solar-Polymer Upcyclers — microplastic becomes a raw material stream, not a pollutant. Microplastic concentrations in human blood, placentas, and breast milk begin measurable decline. Technology cost falls below $0.003/m³; modules are locally manufactured in 80% of deploying countries. The first generation born after 2045 grows up with measurably less plastic in their bodies than any generation since 1950. The plastic age becomes a circular plastic age.
