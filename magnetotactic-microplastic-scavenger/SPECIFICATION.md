## 2. Biological Subsystem

### 2.1 Host Organism

**Species:** *Magnetospirillum magneticum* strain AMB-1 (ATCC 700264)  
**Type:** Gram-negative, microaerophilic, magnetotactic  
**Natural habitat:** freshwater sediments, low-OвӮӮ mud  
**Optimal growth:** 25вҖ“30В°C, pH 6.5вҖ“7.5, dissolved OвӮӮ 1вҖ“3% (microaerophilic)  
**Generation time:** 5вҖ“8 hours (standard), 3вҖ“5 hours (optimized strain)  
**Cell size:** 0.5 Г— 1вҖ“3 Вөm (spirillum morphology)  
**Genome:** 4.7 Mb chromosome + 117 kb magnetosome island (MAI)

### 2.2 Magnetosome Biology

**Magnetosome:** Intracellular membrane-bounded FeвӮғOвӮ„ (magnetite) nanocrystals, arranged in a linear chain along the long cell axis.  
**Wild-type AMB-1:** 6вҖ“20 magnetosomes per cell, 30вҖ“120 nm diameter, total iron 1вҖ“2 fg/cell.  
**Engineered strain (MMS-1):** 12вҖ“30 magnetosomes per cell, 40вҖ“140 nm diameter, total iron 3вҖ“6 fg/cell.  
**Magnetic moment (wild-type):** ~1 Г— 10вҒ»В№вҒө AВ·mВІ  
**Magnetic moment (MMS-1):** ~3вҖ“5 Г— 10вҒ»В№вҒө AВ·mВІ (3вҖ“5Г— enhancement)

**Enhancement engineering:**
- *mamAB* operon (15 genes, core magnetosome formation): cloned under strong *tac* promoter on a low-copy plasmid (pBBR1MCS-2, 5 copies/cell). Increases magnetosome number 1.5вҖ“2Г—.
- *mamGFDC* operon (crystal size control): overexpressed from same plasmid. Increases crystal diameter 20вҖ“40%.
- *feoAB* operon (FeВІвҒә uptake): chromosomal integration under constitutive *mamA* promoter. Increases intracellular iron uptake 2вҖ“3Г—.
- *mms6* (magnetite nucleation): codon-optimized, integrated in MAI. Improves crystallinity and magnetic quality.

### 2.3 Hydrophobin Surface Display

**Display system:** Ice nucleation protein (InaZ) from *Pseudomonas syringae* as outer membrane anchor.

**Construct design:**
```
Promoter: tac (IPTG-inducible, tunable)
Signal peptide: InaZ N-terminal (Sec pathway)
Anchor: InaZ C-terminal repeats (3Г— 21-aa repeat вҶ’ outer membrane insertion)
Fusion: InaZ N-term вҖ” Linker (GGGGS)вӮғ вҖ” Hydrophobin SC3 вҖ” InaZ C-term
Terminator: rrnB T1T2
Plasmid: pBBR1MCS-3 (compatible with magnetosome plasmid, 8 copies/cell)
```

**Hydrophobin choice: SC3 from *Schizophyllum commune***

- 100 aa mature protein, 8 cysteine residues (4 disulfide bridges), amphipathic
- Self-assembles at hydrophobic-hydrophilic interfaces into ~10 nm films
- Binds to polyethylene, polypropylene, polystyrene, PET, PVC, nylon with K_D вүҲ 10вҒ»вҒёвҖ“10вҒ»вҒ№ M
- Stable to pH 2вҖ“10, 4вҖ“60В°C, resistant to proteolysis when assembled
- Well-characterized; recombinant production in E. coli and Pichia demonstrated
- Alternative: HFBI from *Trichoderma reesei* (75 aa, similar properties, smaller, easier to express)

**Surface density:** ~10вҒҙвҖ“10вҒө hydrophobin molecules per cell (limited by InaZ display capacity). This is sufficient for effective binding because hydrophobins self-assemble into patches.

### 2.4 Biocontainment

**Layer 1: Synthetic auxotrophy (dapA deletion)**
- *dapA* gene (dihydrodipicolinate synthase, DAP pathway вҶ’ lysine + DAP) deleted from chromosome
- DAP is required for peptidoglycan cross-linking; without it, cell wall fails вҶ’ lysis
- DAP supplied in regrowth reactor medium (0.3 mg/mL meso-DAP) only
- In the environment (no DAP): cell division fails after 2вҖ“4 generations; lysis within 24вҖ“48 h

**Layer 2: Kill switch (frmR-mazF)**
- *frmR* promoter (formaldehyde-inducible) drives *mazF* (mRNA interferase toxin)
- Threshold: 50 ВөM formaldehyde (environmental background is <1 ВөM in clean water; industrial effluent might trigger)
- Optional: chemical inducer (cumate, anhydrotetracycline) for manual activation
- Activation вҶ’ MazF cleaves cellular mRNA вҶ’ growth arrest + death within 4вҖ“8 h

**Layer 3: Fitness cost**
- Overexpression of *mamAB* + hydrophobin imposes 15вҖ“25% growth penalty vs. wild-type
- In competition with environmental bacteria (no DAP, no selective pressure), MMS-1 is outcompeted within 3вҖ“7 days
- Magnetosome synthesis consumes 20вҖ“30% of cellular iron budget вҖ” severe disadvantage in iron-poor natural waters

## 3. Contact Reactor

### 3.1 Design

- **Type:** Upflow fluidized-bed biofilm reactor
- **Volume:** 2вҖ“5 mВі (for 100вҖ“500 mВі/day WWTP module); 0.5вҖ“2 L (household cartridge)
- **Carrier media:** Recycled glass beads (soda-lime glass), 2вҖ“5 mm diameter, 300 mВІ/mВі specific surface area, sintered for porosity (roughness factor 3вҖ“5Г—)
- **Bed expansion:** 30вҖ“50% at design flow (5вҖ“10 m/h upflow velocity)
- **Bacterial loading:** 10вҒ№вҖ“10В№вҒ° cells per mВІ carrier surface (biofilm + planktonic)
- **Hydraulic residence time:** 2вҖ“5 minutes (sufficient for >90% binding collision probability)
- **Temperature:** 15вҖ“35В°C ambient (no heating required for most climates; 5вҖ“15В°C reduced efficiency 30вҖ“50%)
- **Oxygen:** microaerophilic (1вҖ“3% DO). In WWTP secondary effluent, DO is already 2вҖ“6 mg/L. In anaerobic zones, a low-rate air sparge (0.01вҖ“0.05 vvm) maintains microaerophilic conditions.

### 3.2 Binding Kinetics

**Collision rate (Smoluchowski):**
k_coll = 4ПҖ(D_b + D_p)(r_b + r_p) Г— N_b Г— N_p

Where:
- D_b, D_p = diffusion coefficients of bacterium and particle
- r_b, r_p = radii (bacterium ~0.5 Вөm; particle 0.5вҖ“2500 Вөm)
- N_b, N_p = number densities

For typical WWTP effluent (10вҒ¶вҖ“10вҒ· bacteria/cmВі, 10ВІвҖ“10вҒө particles/cmВі):
- Collision half-time: 30вҖ“120 seconds
- Binding probability on collision: 0.3вҖ“0.8 (hydrophobin-plastic contact)
- Net binding efficiency in 3 min HRT: 85вҖ“95% for >5 Вөm, 65вҖ“85% for 1вҖ“5 Вөm

### 3.3 Biofilm Management

- Fluidization prevents excessive biofilm accumulation (shear controls thickness to 50вҖ“200 Вөm)
- Periodic (weekly) brief aeration increase (10 min at 2Г— flow) sloughs excess biomass
- Biomass is captured in the Halbach column and recycled or bled

## 4. Magnetic Separation Column

### 4.1 Halbach Array Design

**Magnet grade:** NdFeB N52 (B_r = 1.48 T, H_c = 836 kA/m)  
**Array:** 8-segment Halbach cylinder, 10вҖ“20 cm internal diameter, 30вҖ“60 cm length  
**Field achieved:** 1.2вҖ“1.5 T on axis (interior), 0.05вҖ“0.1 T exterior (field confined)  
**Gradient:** 50вҖ“200 T/m across flow channel (radial, toward inner wall)  
**Flow:** Axial (along column length); magnetic force radial (toward wall)

### 4.2 Capture Physics

**Magnetic force on a bacterium-plastic complex:**
F_mag = (m_complex) Г— (вҲҮB)

Where m_complex = n_bacteria Г— m_cell (for n bacteria on one particle)

**Hydrodynamic drag on a complex (Stokes):**
F_drag = 6ПҖ О· r_complex v_flow

**Capture condition:** F_mag > F_drag + gravitational settling (negligible for small particles)

**Operating parameters for >98% capture:**
| Particle size | Bacteria per particle | m_complex (AВ·mВІ) | F_mag (N) | F_drag at 2 m/h (N) | Capture? |
|--------------|---------------------|-------------------|-----------|---------------------|----------|
| 1 Вөm | 1 | 5Г—10вҒ»В№вҒө | 5Г—10вҒ»В№Ві | 2Г—10вҒ»В№Ві | вң“ |
| 5 Вөm | 1 | 5Г—10вҒ»В№вҒө | 5Г—10вҒ»В№Ві | 1Г—10вҒ»В№ВІ | Marginal (need lower flow or more bacteria) |
| 10 Вөm | 2вҖ“3 | 1.5Г—10вҒ»В№вҒҙ | 1.5Г—10вҒ»В№ВІ | 2Г—10вҒ»В№ВІ | Marginal (reduce flow to 1.5 m/h in capture zone) |
| 50 Вөm | 5вҖ“10 | 3Г—10вҒ»В№вҒҙ | 3Г—10вҒ»В№ВІ | 5Г—10вҒ»В№В№ | Need stronger gradient or slower flow |
| 100 Вөm | 10вҖ“20 | 6Г—10вҒ»В№вҒҙ | 6Г—10вҒ»В№ВІ | 1Г—10вҒ»В№вҒ° | Problematic |

**Design solution for large particles:** A two-zone column:
1. **Low-velocity zone** (first 20% of column): v = 0.5вҖ“1.0 m/h, captures all sizes >1 Вөm with вүҘ1 bacterium
2. **Main zone** (remaining 80%): v = 2вҖ“3 m/h, captures smaller complexes; larger complexes already captured

Additionally, a **pre-flocculation step** (gentle stirring in the reactor outlet) encourages bacteria-plastic-bacteria aggregation, increasing n_bacteria per particle for large particles. With 5+ bacteria on a 100 Вөm particle, F_mag = 2.5Г—10вҒ»В№В№ N > F_drag at 2 m/h (1Г—10вҒ»В№вҒ° N вҖ” still marginal). For large particles (>50 Вөm), the system relies on **gravitational settling + magnetic capture synergy**: large particle complexes settle in the low-velocity zone and are scraped/collected at the bottom.

**Practical capture efficiency (modeled):**
- 1вҖ“5 Вөm: 95вҖ“99%
- 5вҖ“20 Вөm: 90вҖ“98%
- 20вҖ“100 Вөm: 85вҖ“95% (settling + magnetic)
- 100 ВөmвҖ“5 mm: 80вҖ“90% (primarily gravitational + magnetic capture in pre-zone)

### 4.3 Column Cleaning

- **Continuous:** 95% of captured material is in the low-velocity zone (first 20% of column)
- **Periodic (every 4вҖ“12 h):** Halbach array rotates 90В° (motorized, 5вҖ“10 seconds) вҶ’ field collapses вҶ’ captured material releases вҶ’ reverse flush at 3Г— flow for 2 minutes вҶ’ concentrate to Stage 3
- **Column life:** NdFeB N52 lifetime >50 years (corrosion-protected by NiCuNi coating); no degradation expected

## 9. Research Frontiers

### 9.1 Nanoplastic Capture (sub-1 Вөm)

The current system targets 1 ВөmвҖ“5 mm. Nanoplastics (<1 Вөm, down to 10 nm) are more abundant, more bioavailable, and potentially more dangerous вҖ” but cannot be captured magnetically with a single bacterium (magnetic moment too low relative to Brownian motion). Research directions:
- **Bacterial aggregation circuits**: engineer c-di-GMP-mediated auto-aggregation to form 10вҖ“50 Вөm bacterial clusters with 100вҖ“500Г— magnetic moment, capable of trapping nanoplastics within the aggregate matrix
- **Extracellular trap proteins**: engineer secretion of hydrophobin-mucin fusion proteins that form a sticky extracellular mesh capturing nanoparticles
- **Nanomagnetite doping**: incorporate CoВІвҒә or MnВІвҒә into magnetosome crystals (CoFeвӮӮOвӮ„, MnFeвӮӮOвӮ„) for 2вҖ“5Г— higher magnetic moment per crystal

### 9.2 Broad-Spectrum Hydrophobin Engineering

- Directed evolution of hydrophobins for higher affinity to specific polymers (e.g., PET-specific hydrophobin with K_D ~10вҒ»В№вҒ° M)
- Multi-hydrophobin display (different hydrophobins for different polymers on the same cell)
- Fusion with plastic-binding peptides (e.g., LCI peptide for PP, Pb_1 for PE) for synergistic binding

### 9.3 In-Situ Magnetic Recovery from Open Water

- Deploy MMS bacteria in constructed wetlands or retention ponds for agricultural runoff microplastic capture
- Use drone-deployed magnetic sweep systems over sediment basins to recover bacteria-plastic complexes
- Integrate with existing water treatment infrastructure without major civil works

### 9.4 Biodegradation Pathway Integration

- Engineer MMS-1 to also express PETase/MHETase (PET-degrading enzymes) on the cell surface

- Challenge: enzyme kinetics vs. binding kinetics (binding is fast; degradation is slow); may require a two-stage bioreactor

### 9.5 Autonomous Deployment & Monitoring
