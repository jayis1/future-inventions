# Ocean Current Turbine

## Problem

Ocean currents (Gulf Stream, Kuroshio, Agulhas) carry 5,000+ GW of kinetic energy constantly — more than humanity's total electricity consumption. But tidal/wave energy devices are limited to coastal areas and suffer from corrosion, biofouling, and extreme storm damage.

## Solution

Deep-ocean submersible turbines anchored to the seafloor that harvest steady ocean currents at 50-200m depth. Unlike surface wave/tidal devices, these operate in the stable, slow-but-constant thermohaline circulation — 24/7 baseload power, no intermittency.

## Key Innovation

- **Helical Darrieus rotor** — omnidirectional, self-starting, no yaw mechanism needed
- **Carbon fiber composite + bio-inspired anti-fouling** — shark-skin riblet texture prevents biofouling without chemicals
- **Seafloor anchor + neutrally buoyant nacelle** — rides the current like a kite, minimal structural loads
- **Subsea DC backbone** — HVDC transmission to shore, 95% efficient at 500km
- **Marine ecosystem co-design** — reef-friendly anchor bases, artificial habitat structures

## Target Cost

$0.03/kWh (cheaper than coal)

## Impact

- 5,000+ GW theoretical extractable — 2x current global electricity
- True baseload renewable — no intermittency, no storage needed
- Invisible from shore — no NIMBY opposition
- Co-located with desalination — freshwater + electricity from same installation
- Marine habitat creation as design feature, not mitigation
