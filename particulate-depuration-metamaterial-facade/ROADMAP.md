# Particulate Depuration Metamaterial Facade — Roadmap

**Version:** 1.0 · **Date:** 2026-06-20

---

## Phase 1 — Concept (2026–2028)

The foundational science is established but unproven as an integrated system. This phase validates each mechanism independently and models their coupling. Lab-scale work includes: (a) characterizing TENG charge output and PM capture efficiency of PTFE/Nylon bimorph meshes in a wind tunnel across 0.5–8 m/s, (b) confirming >90% mineralization of soot/PAH surrogates on TiO₂/rGO/WO₃ coatings under UV-Vis flux matching real urban conditions, and (c) demonstrating that Geobacter/Shewanella biofilms on alginate/GO hydrogels survive intermittent hydration and continue EET-driven oxidation of captured organics. A coupled computational model (COMSOL + CFD + electrostatics + reaction kinetics) predicts integrated performance and identifies the highest-risk interfaces. Deliverable: a peer-reviewed feasibility paper and a detailed integrated-system design specification. **Exit gate:** modeled single-pass capture >80%, mineralization >85%, biofilm stability >30 days.

---

## Phase 2 — Prototype (2028–2031)

Build the first fully integrated 1 m × 2 m panel and test it in a controlled environment chamber simulating urban air (PM₂.₅ 50–150 µg/m³, wind 1–5 m/s, diurnal UV cycle, 20–90% RH). Measure: capture efficiency, mineralization completeness (GC-MS outlet analysis for intermediates — the critical safety test), biofilm viability over 6 months, cleaning-pulse effectiveness, supercapacitor cycle life, and noise absorption coefficient. Iterate on layer formulations (TiO₂:rGO:WO₃ ratio, hydrogel composition, TENG mesh geometry). Subject panels to accelerated weathering (QUV 3,000 hr, thermal cycling −10 to +60°C, simulated acid rain). Deliverable: a validated prototype meeting >85% capture, >90% mineralization, <1% incomplete-oxidation byproducts, and 6-month biofilm stability. Independent biosafety review of the biofilm containment. **Exit gate:** prototype performance within 15% of model prediction; zero detectable hazardous byproducts; biofilm remains BSL-1 contained.

---

## Phase 3 — Pilot (2031–2035)

Deploy 500–2,000 m² of panels in 3–5 real-world pilot sites selected for diversity: a Delhi school courtyard (high PM, arid), a Cairo highway noise barrier (moderate PM, hot), and a Dhaka factory facade (high PM, humid). Each site is instrumented with reference PM monitors (BAM/TEOM) upwind and downwind, VOC/particle-count sensors, and met stations. Run for 18–24 months across all seasons. Measure real-world capture and mineralization, biofilm health under actual climate variability, byproduct emissions, maintenance requirements, and social acceptance. Engage local public-health authorities and WHO for independent air-quality verification. Begin manufacturing-process development for roll-to-roll panel production. Deliverable: peer-reviewed field-study publication, certified performance data, and a manufacturing-ready panel design. **Exit gate:** field-measured PM reduction >40% at pilot scale; confirmed zero hazardous byproducts; biofilm stable across full annual climate cycle; cost projected < $15/m² at scale.

---

## Phase 4 — Scale (2035–2042)

Open-license the panel design to enable local manufacturing in high-PM countries (India, Bangladesh, Pakistan, Nigeria, Indonesia, Egypt). Build 3–5 regional roll-to-roll coating factories (each $20–40M, 5–10 million m²/yr capacity). Launch large-scale retrofit programs funded by climate-health bonds, WHO, World Bank, and national governments: target 50–200 million m² deployed across 50+ cities. Establish an international "Active Facade" certification standard (analogous to LEED) covering performance, biosafety, and byproduct safety. Integrate into building codes for new construction in PM non-attainment zones. Deliverable: 100+ cities with measurable PM₂.₅ reductions of 30–60%, independently verified; manufacturing cost < $10/m²; 5+ regional factories operating. **Exit gate:** independent public-health studies confirm 200K–500K deaths/year being prevented; black carbon forcing measurably reduced in deployment regions.

---

## Phase 5 — Ubiquity (2042–2050)

PDMF becomes standard building material in PM-affected regions — as automatic as insulation in cold climates. Building codes worldwide require active-facade provisions for new construction in non-attainment zones. Retrofit programs complete coverage of existing stock in the highest-PM cities. Total deployed surface exceeds 2 billion m², treating the lower atmosphere of every major city multiple times daily. The PDMF platform spawns derivatives: CO₂-mineralizing facades, pathogen-sensing facades, humidity-harvesting facades — the "metabolic building" paradigm. Local manufacturing employs tens of thousands across the Global South. The technology is fully open-source with active global research communities improving performance and cost. Ambient PM₂.₅ in deployed cities drops below WHO Interim Target 3 (15 µg/m³) for 80%+ of days. **Outcome:** 800K–2.0 million premature deaths prevented annually; 0.2–0.6 Gt CO₂-eq/yr black carbon forcing eliminated; the city-as-lung is reality.
