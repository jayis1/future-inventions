## 3. Ecological Impact

### Revegetation Trajectory (Per Module)

| Timeframe | Vegetation Cover | Carbon Sequestration (biological) | Soil Stability |
|-----------|-----------------|----------------------------------|----------------|
| Year 0–1 | Bare biochar/perlite | 0 (establishment) | Mechanical only |
| Year 1–5 | Grasses + sedges (20–50% cover) | 0.1–0.5 kg C/m²/yr | Root stabilization begins |
| Year 5–15 | Mosses colonize (50–80% cover) | 0.3–1.0 kg C/m²/yr | Active layer stabilizing |
| Year 15–30 | Peat layer forming (80–100% cover) | 0.5–1.5 kg C/m²/yr | Self-sustaining ecosystem |
| Year 30+ | Mature moss/peat (κ ≈ 0.02 W/m·K) | 1.0–2.0 kg C/m²/yr | Mechanical cooling reducible |

### At Ubiquity (10M modules, 1,000 km²)

- **Biological carbon sequestration:** 50–150 kt C/yr once peat establishes (Year 15+)
- **Habitat restored:** 1,000 km² of degraded tundra → functioning wetland/moss ecosystem
- **Hydrology restored:** Permafrost-dependent thermokarst lakes and wetlands stabilized
- **Biodiversity:** Native Arctic flora and mycorrhizal networks re-established across protected zones

## 6. Summary

The PTSA addresses a **unique, non-substitutable** climate risk — the permafrost-carbon feedback loop — at a cost ($10–40/t CO₂-eq) competitive with the cheapest available interventions. Its passive, zero-operating-energy design makes it deployable in the most remote Arctic regions without infrastructure. Its revegetation collar converts a mechanical fix into permanent ecological restoration. At full scale, it can **avoid 0.1–0.3°C of warming by 2100**, protect **$80–100B in infrastructure**, and create a **new $1–5B/year carbon finance sector**. The ROI, even conservatively estimated, exceeds 50:1 over 50 years.

**The sleeping giant can be kept asleep — for $2–6 per square meter.**
