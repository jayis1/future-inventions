# Phononic Thermoelectric Harvester — Roadmap

## Phase 1 — Concept (2026–2027)

Computational validation of the phononic crystal bandgap in SnSe/Mo and Mg₂Si/TiO₂ layer systems using density functional perturbation theory (DFPT) and the Boltzmann transport equation. Molecular dynamics simulations of phonon-electron scattering at interfaces. Down-selection of optimal layer pairs, thicknesses, and period counts. Publication of predicted ZT in a peer-reviewed journal. Formation of a core team of 5–8 researchers (materials science, thermal engineering, power electronics). Initial IP filings on the phononic filter architecture and graded contact design. Budget: $2–3M (government grant + venture pre-seed).

## Phase 2 — Prototype (2027–2030)

Fabrication of first single-leg PTH devices at lab scale: sputter-deposit 10–20 period SnSe/Mo phononic filters on SPS-sintered nanostructured SnSe substrates. Measure κ_lattice, σ, S, and ZT from 300–800 K. Target: demonstrate ZT ≥ 3.0 in a single leg at ΔT = 300 K, confirming the phononic bandgap reduces κ by ≥5× while preserving σ. Build first 1 W tile (4 legs), validate module-level efficiency ≥15%. Address SnSe oxidation with hermetic sealing. Develop roll-to-roll sputtering process for filter deposition. Budget: $8–15M (Series A / DOE ARPA-E).

## Phase 3 — Pilot (2030–2033)

Deploy 10–50 pilot installations at industrial partner sites (steel mill, cement plant, glass factory) totaling 50–200 kW. Validate real-world efficiency (target 12–18% in field conditions), durability (5,000 thermal cycles, 8,000 hr continuous operation), and cost (target ≤$3/W at 10K-unit pilot line). Launch cookstove pilot in 3 countries (1,000 units) measuring daily energy harvest, battery reliability, and user adoption. Establish manufacturing process for flexible wrap form factor. Secure UL/IEC safety certification. Budget: $30–60M (Series B + industrial partner co-investment).

## Phase 4 — Scale (2033–2038)

Build first 1M-unit/year manufacturing line (roll-to-roll sputtering + SPS + automated dicing + packaging) at $50–80M capex, driving module cost to ≤$1.50/W. Deploy 5–10 GW of industrial PTH capacity across steel, cement, glass, chemicals, refining. Scale cookstove program to 10M units across 20 countries via microfinance and climate finance. Integrate with heavy-duty truck and ship exhaust programs (OEM partnerships). Begin utility-scale pilot: 10 MW PTH array on a 500 MW gas plant's cooling loop. Revenue: $200–500M/year. Budget: $150–300M (Series C / strategic investment).

## Phase 5 — Ubiquity (2038–2045)

PTH becomes standard infrastructure. Building codes require waste-heat recovery on new industrial construction. 50M+ modules deployed globally; cumulative installed capacity 50–150 GW. Cost reaches $0.50/W at 10M-unit/year scale. Cookstove program reaches 100M households — 500M people electrified. Thermoelectric manufacturing is a $50–100B/year industry employing 1–2M people. Circular recycling established for Sn/Se recovery. The Wiedemann-Franz law is obsolete; phononic thermal filtering is taught in undergraduate solid-state physics. Waste heat is no longer "waste."
