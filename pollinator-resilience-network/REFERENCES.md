# Pollinator Resilience Network — References

## Bee Gut Microbiome & Engineering

1. **Kwong, W.K., et al.** (2014). "Genome of the honey bee gut symbiont *Snodgrassella alvi*." *Genome Announcements* 2(3). DOI: 10.1128/genomeA.00257-14
   — Type strain genome, basis for BeeShield chassis.

2. **Kwong, W.K., et al.** (2017). "Dynamic microbiome in honeybees." *ISME Journal* 11: 973–984.
   — Host specificity of bee gut symbionts.

3. **Leonard, S.P., et al.** (2020). "Engineered symbiont activity against an RNA virus and a DNA virus in bumblebees." *Science* 367: 573–576.
   — Engineered *Snodgrassella alvi* confers immune priming; foundation for BeeShield dsRNA module.

4. **Leonard, S.P., et al.** (2018). "Engineered *S. alvi* delivers dsRNA to honey bees." *PNAS* 115(48): 12130–12135.
   — dsRNA delivery via engineered gut symbiont demonstrates RNAi in bees.

5. **Raymann, K., & Moran, N.A.** (2018). "The role of the gut microbiome in health and disease of honey bees." *Current Opinion in Insect Science* 26: 45–51.
   — Core gut microbiome composition and function.

## Neonicotinoid Resistance (CYP9Q)

6. **Manjon, F.R., et al.** (2018). "CYP9Q-mediated neonicotinoid resistance in honeybees." *PNAS* 115(51): E13010.
   — CYP9Q family metabolizes neonicotinoids; basis for CYP9Q4 expression cassette.

7. **Goulson, D.** (2013). "An overview of the environmental risks posed by neonicotinoid insecticides." *Journal of Applied Ecology* 50: 977–987.
   — Sublethal neonicotinoid effects on bee navigation and foraging.

9. **Nunes, F.M.F., et al.** (2013). "The bee pathogen *Nosema ceranae*." *PLoS ONE* 8(10).

10. **Ramsey, S.D., et al.** (2019). "Varroa destructor feeds primarily on honey bee fat body tissue and not hemolymph." *PNAS* 116(5): 1792–1801.

## Pesticide Drift & Rhizoremediation
