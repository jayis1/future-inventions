# Roadmap — Pollinator Resilience Network

## Phase 1: Concept (2025–2026)

Foundational research and proof-of-concept validation. Academic labs (Moran lab, Harvard Wyss, Rothamsted Research) demonstrate individual system components in controlled conditions: CYP9Q4 expression in *S. alvi* achieving ≥10× neonicotinoid burden reduction in caged bees; dsRNA cocktails achieving ≥70% Varroa reproduction suppression in mini-hives; first HiveSentinel prototype achieving >75% classification accuracy on 100 hive audio datasets. CorridorMapper v0.1 runs on open Sentinel-2 data for one pilot region (Yolo County, CA). Open-source release of all protocols. Regulatory pre-submission meetings with EPA, EFSA. **Gate:** peer-reviewed replication of BeeShield™ efficacy in ≥3 independent labs.

## Phase 2: Prototype (2026–2028)

Integrate the three BeeShield™ modules into a single *S. alvi* chassis with biocontainment (DAP auxotrophy). Field-scale trial in 50–200 hives across 3 climatic zones (CA Central Valley, Bavaria, Yucatán) measuring colony survival, Varroa load, DWV titer, and neonicotinoid residue over one full season. DriftShield cultivars bred to T2 (3× trichome density, 5× charge retention) and tested at 5 field sites. HiveSentinel v1.0 produced at $12 BOM, deployed at 500 nodes across pilot regions — first landscape-scale acoustic health maps generated. CorridorMapper v1.0 adds drone imagery and graph-theory optimization. **Gate:** colony loss rate reduced to <20% in treatment vs. 35% control across all sites.

## Phase 3: Pilot (2028–2031)

Multi-region deployment across 5,000–20,000 hives and 10,000–50,000 hectares in 4 countries. Full four-layer PRN deployed end-to-end with closed feedback loop (HiveSentinel → CorridorMapper → DriftShield → BeeShield). Community-managed governance model established via pollinator cooperatives. Regulatory approval secured in ≥2 jurisdictions (EU, US). Cost-per-hectare driven to $50–80. HiveSentinel BOM reduced to $5. Open-source firmware and model weights released. First wild-pollinator BeeShield™ delivery via nectar feeders tested at 10 forest-edge sites. **Gate:** independently audited colony loss <15%, wild pollinator diversity +30% vs. baseline at pilot sites.

## Phase 4: Scale (2031–2038)

National-scale deployment in 15–30 countries covering 50–200M hectares of pollinator-dependent cropland. 5–10M HiveSentinel nodes operational globally. Regional BeeShield™ production hubs (fermentation + formulation) established on 4 continents. CorridorMapper integrated into national agricultural data systems (USDA, EU CAP, FAO). DriftShield cultivars available through 100+ regional seed cooperatives. Total pollinator-service loss reduction of $30–80B/year realized. Beekeeper colony loss rate globally below 15%. **Gate:** UN-recognized global pollinator health index derived from PRN network data.

## Phase 5: Ubiquity (2038–2050)

Universal adoption in all pollinator-dependent agricultural regions. 50M+ HiveSentinel nodes form a planetary-scale ecological monitoring network. BeeShield™ is standard practice — like vaccination for livestock. DriftShield hedgerows are a regulatory requirement in agricultural policy worldwide. CorridorMapper is the default planning tool for every agricultural landscape. Colony loss rates normalized to 5–10% (weather-driven, not crisis-driven). 20,000+ pollinator species in stable or recovering populations. $150–400B/year in pollinator-service losses prevented. The pollinator crisis is solved.
