# RAR — Development Roadmap

## Phase 1: Concept (2026–2028)

Materials science and thermodynamic cycle validation. Optimize the CaCl₂-biochar composite (uptake, kinetics, 1,000-cycle stability) via gravimetric vapor sorption testing. Characterize radiative cooling coatings (SiO₂/PDMS film and BaSO₄ paint) under indoor solar simulator + outdoor conditions. Build benchtop subsystem test rigs: adsorbent bed with flat-plate collector, radiatively-cooled condenser, evaporator with ice storage. Integrate into a 20 L lab prototype and measure COP under controlled ambient (15–40°C) and solar (200–1,000 W/m²) conditions. Target: demonstrate COP ≥0.45, interior 2–8°C, ice production. Publish peer-reviewed validation. **TRL 2 → TRL 3.**

## Phase 2: Prototype (2028–2030)

Build 20 full-scale 100 L prototypes with passive valves, VIP insulation, and integrated collector/condenser/evaporator. Deploy to 5 field sites: Kenya (Marsabit, hot dry), India (Gujarat, hot humid), Bangladesh (Sirajganj, humid subtropical), Senegal (Saint-Louis, Sahelian), and Indonesia (West Timor, tropical). Run 6-month field trials measuring interior temperature, COP, ice production, holdover, valve reliability, and user interaction. Simultaneously initiate WHO PQS Category 4.3 pre-qualification testing (freeze protection, hot-zone 43°C, holdover, cold-zone). Iterate on valve design, ice management, and insulation durability. Target: COP ≥0.50, 24 h holdover, 95% uptime, interior never outside 2–8°C for >2 h continuous. **TRL 3 → TRL 5.**

## Phase 3: Pilot (2030–2032)

Establish pilot assembly lines in Kenya and India (10,000 units/year capacity each). Forge microfinance partnerships (Kiva, local MFIs) for household units ($10/month × 12 months). Secure WHO PQS Category 4.3 certification for the 200 L health-post variant. Distribute 1,000 health-post units via Gavi/UNICEF/Ministry of Health programs in 5 countries. Deploy 5,000 household and community units through agricultural cooperatives and NGOs. Collect real-world failure data, maintenance costs, and user satisfaction. Refine manufacturing process for cost reduction (target $120 average). Begin local biochar adsorbent production at each assembly site. **TRL 5 → TRL 6. 10,000+ units deployed.**

## Phase 4: Scale (2032–2036)

Expand regional manufacturing to 5 countries (Kenya, India, Indonesia, Nigeria, Brazil) with 100,000 units/year capacity per region. Diversify product line: 30 L household, 100 L standard, 200 L health-post, 500 L community cold store. Launch fisheries cold-chain programs (50M small-scale fishers target), dairy cooperative programs, and horticulture perishable storage programs. Integrate with mobile cold-chain logistics — RAR as the "cold node" in last-mile distribution networks. Achieve <2% annual failure rate and $100 average unit cost. Develop adsorbent recharge service network (500 technicians per region). Begin tech transfer for radiative coating and VIP production to regional manufacturers. **TRL 6 → TRL 7. 500,000+ units deployed.**

## Phase 5: Ubiquity (2036–2045)

Global mass deployment: 1M+ units/year production across 20+ manufacturing countries. Cumulative 50–500M units deployed. Unit cost falls to $60–80 (household) through economies of scale and local content reaching 95%+. The RAR becomes the default off-grid refrigeration technology — displacing kerosene absorption entirely and capturing the majority of new off-grid cold-chain installations. HFC-based off-grid refrigeration is phased out in 50+ countries via policy mandates favoring zero-GWP alternatives. The adsorbent recharge industry employs 500,000+ technicians globally. RAR units collectively save 200–400 Mt food/year, 50M+ vaccine doses/year, and avoid 0.5–2.0 Gt CO₂-eq/year. Refrigeration is recognized as a universal basic service. **TRL 7 → TRL 9. 500M+ units. The cold chain serves everyone.**
