| Metric | Current Annual Loss | SMIF-Mitigated Loss (at scale) | Savings |
|--------|-------------------|-------------------------------|---------|
| Direct building damage | $30–80B/yr | $3–16B/yr | $14–77B/yr |
| Business interruption | $10–40B/yr | $1–8B/yr | $2–39B/yr |
| Emergency response & relief | $5–20B/yr | $0.5–4B/yr | $1–19.5B/yr |
| Reconstruction | $10–30B/yr | $1–6B/yr | $4–29B/yr |
| **Total** | **$55–170B/yr** | **$5.5–34B/yr** | **$21–164.5B/yr** |

**Cost-benefit:** At $40–80/m² per building and 272M at-risk buildings, full deployment costs ~$650B–1,300B. Annual savings of $21–164B yields payback in **4–60 years** — well within the 50+ year SMIF service life. For high-risk regions (Turkey, Nepal, Perú), payback is <10 years.

## 7. Key Uncertainties

1. **Field validation gap:** No full-scale SMIF has been tested in a real earthquake. Simulated 80–95% attenuation may be 60–85% in practice — still transformative but requires empirical calibration.
2. **Geopolymer long-term durability:** 50-year underground performance extrapolated from accelerated testing; real-world validation needed over 10–20 year horizons.
3. **Manufacturing scale-up:** Auxetic lattice at $15–25/m² requires large-format 3D printing maturity that may not arrive until 2032–2035.
4. **Code adoption timeline:** 5–7 years per jurisdiction; global coverage requires 15–20 years of sustained advocacy.
5. **Behavioral adoption:** Even at low cost, voluntary uptake requires education, trust, and demonstration — government mandates may be necessary for universal coverage.
