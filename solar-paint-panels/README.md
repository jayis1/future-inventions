# Solar Paint Panels

## Problem

Solar panels require manufacturing plants, heavy installation, and structural roof support. 60% of building surfaces (walls, curved surfaces, irregular roofs) cannot accommodate traditional panels. The installed cost ($2.50-3.50/W) keeps solar inaccessible for many.

## Solution

A photovoltaic paint containing perovskite nanocrystals that can be sprayed onto any surface — walls, roofs, vehicles, fences — turning them into solar generators. Applied with standard paint equipment, cures in 24 hours, generates electricity for 15+ years.

## Key Innovation

- **Perovskite nanocrystal ink** — CH₃NH₃PbI₃ nanocrystals suspended in polymer binder, sprayable
- **Self-healing polymer matrix** — repairs micro-cracks from thermal cycling via dynamic covalent bonds
- **Transparent top electrode** — PEDOT:PSS + silver nanowire mesh, 90% transparent, 15 Ω/sq
- **Roll-to-roll manufactured paint cartridges** — $0.30/W at scale (10x cheaper than silicon panels)
- **Any surface, any shape** — flat, curved, vertical, inverted — if sunlight hits it, it generates

## Target Cost

$0.30/W installed (vs $2.50/W traditional solar)

## Impact

- 10x more surface area available for solar capture globally
- 10x cheaper installation — paint it on, wire it up
- Unlocks solar for renters, developing nations, informal housing
- Aesthetic freedom — available in colors (tunable bandgap perovskites)
- Can retrofit existing buildings without structural assessment
