# Solar-Polymer Upcycler

## Problem

Humanity produces over **380 million tonnes of plastic per year**, yet only ~9% is recycled. The rest is landfilled, incinerated, or leaks into oceans and soils. Current mechanical recycling degrades polymer quality each cycle ("downcycling"), while chemical recycling (pyrolysis, solvolysis) requires 400–800 °C temperatures, fossil-fuel heating, and centralized multi-billion-dollar plants — leaving most of the world's plastic waste untreated. Mixed, contaminated, and multi-layer packaging (which makes up >60% of consumer plastic) is essentially unrecyclable by today's methods. The result: 11 million tonnes of plastic enter the ocean annually; microplastics are found in human blood, placentas, and drinking water worldwide.

## Solution

The **Solar-Polymer Upcycler (SPU)** is a community-scale, solar-powered catalytic depolymerization system that converts mixed, contaminated plastic waste into **virgin-quality monomers** (ethylene, propylene, styrene, terephthalic acid, caprolactam) using photothermal **metal-organic frameworks (MOFs)** and engineered depolymerase enzymes. The system operates at 150–250 °C (vs. 400–800 °C for pyrolysis), powered entirely by concentrated sunlight and photovoltaic energy, with no fossil-fuel input. It accepts unsorted, contaminated, multi-layer waste — the kind current recycling cannot touch — and outputs separable, refinery-ready monomers.

### How It Works

1. **Feedstock Handling**: Shredded mixed plastic (≤5 mm flakes) enters a pre-wash chamber that removes >95% of organic contaminants using ultrasonic cavitation in recycled water.
2. **Photothermal Catalysis**: The pre-washed flakes enter a flow reactor lined with **Zr₆-based MOF-808 derivatives** functionalized with Lewis-acid zirconium nodes and anchored **engineered depolymerase-mimetic catalytic sites**. Concentrated solar thermal energy (via compound parabolic concentrators, CPCs) heats the reactor to 150–250 °C. The MOF's photothermal absorption (broadband 300–2500 nm, >92% solar absorptance) converts sunlight to heat with near-unity efficiency, maintaining reaction temperature without external fuel.
3. **Selective Depolymerization**: The dual catalytic system works in tandem:
   - **MOF Lewis-acid sites** cleave C–C and C–O bonds in polyolefins (PE, PP) and polyesters (PET) via retro-insertion and hydrolysis pathways at 180–250 °C.
   - **Enzyme-mimetic sites** (engineered PETase/PHL7 variants immobilized on MOF pores via covalent linker chemistry) depolymerize PET, PLA, and polyamides at 60–150 °C in a staged cascade zone, achieving >90% monomer recovery.
4. **Monomer Separation**: Depolymerized products enter a fractional distillation column (solar-heated) that separates monomers by boiling point. A membrane separator (polyimide hollow-fiber) isolates gases (ethylene b.p. −104 °C, propylene b.p. −48 °C) from liquids (styrene b.p. 145 °C, caprolactam b.p. 270 °C). Terephthalic acid precipitates and is filtered.
5. **Catalyst Regeneration**: The MOF catalyst bed is regenerated in-situ every 50–100 hours by a solar-thermal oxidation cycle (300 °C, 30 min) that burns off coke deposits while preserving MOF structure — verified by in-situ XRD monitoring.

## Key Innovation

**Photothermal MOF dual-catalysis** — combining broadband solar-absorbing MOF-808 derivatives (providing both the heat source and Lewis-acid catalytic sites) with immobilized engineered depolymerase-mimetic enzymes in a single reactor architecture. This eliminates the need for: (a) external fossil-fuel heating, (b) high-temperature pyrolysis, and (c) pure, sorted feedstock. The MOF serves triple duty as photothermal absorber, catalyst scaffold, and enzyme host — achieving depolymerization at 150–250 °C (vs. 400–800 °C conventional) with >90% monomer selectivity on mixed waste.

## Impact

- **Plastic waste eliminated**: Up to 500 tonnes/year per unit; 1M units deployed by 2040 → 500M tonnes/year (exceeds current annual production)
- **Ocean plastic reduction**: Cuts input by >80% when deployed in coastal cities
- **Microplastic prevention**: Eliminates downcycling loop; true closed-loop recycling
- **Climate**: Avoids 1.5–2.5 tonnes CO₂ per tonne plastic recycled (vs. incineration); total potential: 750M–1.25B tonnes CO₂/year avoided
- **Democratization**: Community-scale deployment (500 kg/day fits in a shipping container) makes recycling accessible to small cities, islands, and developing regions
- **Economic**: Virgin-quality monomers command $1.00–2.50/kg; generates $150K–300K/year revenue per unit
- **Jobs**: 2–3 operators per unit → 2–3M jobs globally at scale
