# Solar-Polymer Upcycler — Development Roadmap

**From lab curiosity to global waste infrastructure: a 20-year plan to eliminate plastic waste.**

---

## Overview

The SPU development roadmap spans five phases, each with clear deliverables, go/no-go gates, and scale targets. The philosophy is **"prove it works before you scale it"** — every phase must demonstrate specific performance thresholds before the next phase receives funding.

Total estimated R&D investment: $150–250M over 20 years.
Estimated ROI at full scale: 10–50× (based on monomer sales revenue from 1M+ units).

---

## Phase 1: Lab Validation (2026–2028)

**Goal:** Prove the core science — photothermal MOF catalysis works, enzyme-MOF composites are stable, depolymerization of mixed waste is achievable.

### Milestones

| # | Milestone | Target Date | Success Criteria | Go/No-Go Gate |
|---|-----------|-------------|------------------|---------------|
| 1.1 | Synthesize MOF-808-DP and characterize photothermal properties | Q2 2027 | α > 0.90 (300–2500 nm); η_pt > 90% at 10× solar concentration | Must achieve >0.85 α to continue |
| 1.2 | Demonstrate PHL7 immobilization on MOF-808-DP | Q3 2027 | Enzyme loading >3 wt%; activity retention >70% vs. free enzyme | Must retain >50% activity |
| 1.3 | PET depolymerization with immobilized enzyme | Q4 2027 | >90% PET conversion to TPA + EG at 65–72 °C in 24 h | Must achieve >80% |
| 1.4 | Polyolefin depolymerization with MOF Lewis-acid sites | Q2 2028 | >70% PE/PP conversion to C₂–C₅ olefins at 200–250 °C | Must achieve >60% |
| 1.5 | Benchtop photothermal reactor (100 g/day) | Q4 2028 | Solar-driven operation, no external heat; >50% conversion on 3-polymer mix | Must demonstrate solar self-heating |
| 1.6 | Catalyst regeneration demonstration | Q4 2028 | 10 regeneration cycles with <10% activity loss | Must achieve <20% loss |
| 1.7 | IP protection | Q4 2028 | File 3+ provisional patents (MOF composition, reactor design, enzyme immobilization) | — |
| 1.8 | Publication | Q4 2028 | 2+ peer-reviewed publications | — |

### Budget: $5–8M

| Item | Cost |
|------|------|
| Lab personnel (8 FTE) | $3.2M |
| Equipment (reactor, analytical) | $1.5M |
| MOF synthesis materials | $0.8M |
| Enzyme engineering (computational + experimental) | $1.0M |
| Patent filing | $0.3M |
| Overhead | $1.2M |

### Key Risks

- MOF photothermal performance falls short → pivot to plasmonic nanoparticle enhancers
- Enzyme immobilization kills activity → try alternative linkers (click chemistry, sortase-mediated)
- Lewis-acid sites insufficient for polyolefins → explore co-catalyst (Ni, Pt nanoparticles)

## Phase 2: Pilot System (2028–2031)

**Goal:** Build a 50 kg/day pilot system that processes real mixed waste. Demonstrate 100+ hour continuous operation. Field test in 2 communities.

### Milestones

| # | Milestone | Target Date | Success Criteria | Go/No-Go Gate |
|---|-----------|-------------|------------------|---------------|
| 2.1 | Scale MOF synthesis to 10 kg batches | Q2 2029 | MOF cost <$200/kg; consistent quality across batches | Must achieve <25% batch-to-batch variation |
| 2.2 | Engineer PHL7 variant with T₁ₘ > 85 °C | Q4 2029 | 8–12 mutations; >2× activity at 85 °C vs. wild-type at 65 °C | Must achieve T₁ₘ > 80 °C |
| 2.3 | Build 50 kg/day pilot unit | Q2 2030 | All 5 subsystems integrated in container | Must pass pressure test and safety review |
| 2.4 | Achieve >75% conversion on 3+ polymer blend | Q4 2030 | Real MSW feedstock, not lab-prepared mixtures | Must achieve >65% |
| 2.5 | 100+ hour continuous operation | Q1 2031 | No unscheduled shutdowns; in-spec monomer output | Must achieve 80+ hours |
| 2.6 | 500+ catalyst regeneration cycles | Q2 2031 | <5% cumulative activity loss | Must achieve <10% loss |
| 2.7 | Field test: coastal community (Manila area) | Q3 2031 | 3-month deployment; >70% uptime; local operator training | Must achieve >60% uptime |
| 2.8 | Field test: inland community | Q4 2031 | 3-month deployment; solar intermittency handling | Must demonstrate PCM buffer adequacy |

### Budget: $15–25M

| Item | Cost |
|------|------|
| Engineering team (15 FTE) | $7.5M |
| Pilot hardware (2 units) | $4.0M |
| MOF scale-up (continuous flow synthesis rig) | $3.0M |
| Enzyme production (100 L fermentation) | $1.5M |
| Field test logistics | $2.0M |
| Regulatory engagement | $1.0M |
| Overhead | $5.0M |

### Key Decisions

- **Container vs. skid-mounted**: Container is preferred for mobility but may limit reactor size. Evaluate during pilot.
- **N₂ generator vs. bottled N₂**: PSA generator adds CAPEX but reduces OPEX. Evaluate OPEX tradeoff.
- **Open-source vs. proprietary catalyst**: Open-source accelerates adoption; proprietary protects IP. Decision at Phase 2 gate.

### Business Model

| Model | Description | Revenue Stream |
|-------|-------------|----------------|
| **Direct sale** | Customer buys SPU outright | Hardware + installation + training |
| **Lease** | Monthly lease ($2,000–4,000/month) | Equipment + maintenance contract |
| **Monomer purchase agreement** | SPU installed free; operator sells monomers to us | Monomer offtake at 60–80% market price |
| **Franchise** | Municipal franchise with territory exclusivity | Franchise fee + spare parts + catalyst supply |
| **Carbon credit** | SPU qualifies for plastic credits (Verra Plastic Standard) | Credit revenue shared with operator |

## Phase 5: Ubiquity (2038–2045)

**Goal:** 1M units deployed → 500M tonnes/year capacity. Integration with municipal waste streams worldwide. Closed-loop plastic economy achieved in participating regions.

### Milestones

| # | Milestone | Target Date | Success Criteria |
|---|-----------|-------------|------------------|
| 5.1 | 100,000 units deployed | Q4 2040 | 50M tonnes/year capacity; 100+ countries |
| 5.2 | MOF cost reaches $30/kg | Q2 2041 | At 100,000-unit/year production |
| 5.3 | SPU unit cost <$60K | Q4 2041 | At volume; enables developing-nation deployment without subsidies |
| 5.4 | Ocean plastic input reduced 50% from peak | Q4 2042 | Measured by ocean monitoring networks |
| 5.5 | 1M units deployed | Q4 2045 | 500M tonnes/year capacity; exceeds current annual plastic production |
| 5.6 | Closed-loop circular economy in 50+ regions | Q4 2045 | New plastic = recycled plastic (monomer → polymer → product → waste → SPU → monomer) |
| 5.7 | Next-gen SPU: PFAS destruction capability | Q4 2045 | Modified MOF-808 breaks C–F bonds at 300–400 °C; addresses forever chemicals |
| 5.8 | Plastic waste classified as "raw material" in 100+ jurisdictions | Q4 2045 | Regulatory recognition; eliminates landfilling |

### Long-Term Vision (2045–2060)

- **SPU-2000**: 2 tonnes/day in same container; modular "stackable" design
- **Shipboard SPUs**: Autonomous vessels harvest ocean plastic and process at sea
- **SPU-PFAS**: Dedicated PFAS destruction units co-located at wastewater treatment plants
- **SPU-Textile**: Variant optimized for synthetic textile waste (polyester, nylon, acrylic)
- **Bio-MOF successors**: MOF catalysts grown from engineered microorganisms (carbon-neutral manufacturing)
- **Global plastic treaty**: SPU infrastructure enables legally binding plastic circularity mandates
