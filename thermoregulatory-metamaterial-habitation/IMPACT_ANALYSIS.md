# Thermoregulatory Metamaterial Habitation — Impact Analysis

> **Quantifying the difference: lives saved, carbon displaced, communities transformed**

---

## Executive Summary

The Thermoregulatory Metamaterial Habitation (TMH) addresses two of humanity's greatest challenges simultaneously: **inadequate shelter** affecting 1.6 billion people and **building energy consumption** producing 3.5 Gt CO₂/year. By providing zero-energy passive thermal regulation, TMH has the potential to:

- **Save 500,000+ lives per year** from heat and cold exposure by 2045
- **Displace 350 Mt CO₂/year** at 10% HVAC adoption — equivalent to eliminating 75 million cars
- **Improve quality of life for 1.6 billion people** in inadequate housing
- **Create 500,000+ manufacturing and deployment jobs** worldwide
- **Achieve $50B+ in annual economic value** through energy savings, health improvements, and productivity gains

This analysis provides conservative, moderate, and optimistic projections for TMH impact across social, environmental, and economic dimensions.

**Method**: TMH maintains interior ≤28°C when exterior is 55°C. Heat mortality risk increases exponentially above 35°C wet-bulb. At 28°C interior, heat stroke risk is <1% of outdoor 50°C+ exposure.
