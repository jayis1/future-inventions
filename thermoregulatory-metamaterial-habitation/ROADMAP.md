## Phase 1: Concept Validation (2026–2028)

**Goal:** Prove the physics works. Demonstrate that gallium-alloy micro-bridges can switch thermal conductivity by 10× and that the four-layer system achieves net-zero-energy thermal regulation.

### Year 1 (2026–2027): Component-Level Research

| Quarter | Milestone | Deliverable | Team |
|---------|-----------|-------------|------|
| Q1 | Gallium alloy composition finalized | Ga₆₈.₅In₂₁.₅Sn₁₀ alloy, Tm = 28.8°C, characterized | Metallurgy (3 researchers) |
| Q2 | Micro-channel fabrication demonstrated | 100μm channels, 200/cm² density, in HDPE | Materials science (3 researchers) |
| Q3 | Vacuum alloy injection process validated | Clean filling, >95% channel fill rate | Manufacturing engineering (2) |
| Q4 | TSI switching ratio measured | κ_eff = 0.5 (cold) / 0.03 (hot) = 16.7× ±2× | Thermal physics (2) |

**Year 1 budget: $2.5M** (personnel $1.5M, equipment $0.7M, materials $0.3M)

### Year 2 (2027–2028): Integrated Layer Testing

| Quarter | Milestone | Deliverable | Team |
|---------|-----------|-------------|------|
| Q1 | Radiative coating benchmarked | R > 0.96, ε > 0.95, cooling 100 W/m² demonstrated | Optics (2) |
| Q2 | PCM microcapsule cycling completed | 10,000 cycles, <5% capacity loss | Chemical engineering (2) |
| Q3 | IR-selective emitter fabricated | ε switching 0.25↔0.82 demonstrated | Thin-film (2) |
| Q4 | First 4-layer mini-panel (20×20 cm) tested | Interior 18–26°C across -20°C to +45°C exterior | Full team (5) |

**Year 2 budget: $3.0M** (personnel $1.8M, equipment $0.8M, materials $0.4M)  
**Cumulative: $5.5M**

### Phase 1 Decision Gates

| Gate | Criterion | Go/No-Go |
|------|-----------|----------|

## Phase 3: Pilot Deployment (2030–2032)

**Goal:** Deploy 500 shelters across 5 climate zones. Validate real-world performance. Build humanitarian partnerships. Begin building code certification.

### Year 5 (2030–2031): First Deployments

| Site | Shelters | Climate | Partner | Focus |
|------|---------|---------|---------|-------|
| Diffa, Niger | 100 | Hot desert (45°C+ days) | Niger Red Cross | Heat survival |
| Iqaluit, Canada | 50 | Arctic (-40°C winters) | Nunavut Housing Corp | Cold survival |
| Mumbai, India | 100 | Tropical monsoon (35°C, 90% RH) | IIT Bombay + BRAC | Humidity management |
| Phoenix, USA | 50 | Hot arid (48°C) | ASU + FEMA | Urban heat island |
| Cox's Bazar, Bangladesh | 100 | Tropical + cyclone risk | UNHCR | Refugee shelter |
| Dhaka, Bangladesh | 50 | Urban slum heat | BRAC | Retrofit deployment |
| Christchurch, NZ | 50 | Temperate + seismic | Red Cross | Earthquake response |

**Total: 500 shelters, 17,000 panels**

| Activity | Timeline | Deliverable |
|----------|----------|-------------|

## Risk Management Timeline

| Risk | Phase When Critical | Mitigation Investment | Trigger for Action |
|------|---------------------|----------------------|-------------------|
| Gallium supply shortage | Phase 4–5 | $10M in extraction R&D (Phase 3) | Market price >$300/kg sustained |
| Indium supply constraint | Phase 4 | $5M in LCD recycling (Phase 3) | Supply <80% of demand |
| Fire certification failure | Phase 2 | $2M in alternative flame retardants (Phase 1–2) | V-0 not achieved after 3 attempts |
| TSI degradation faster than expected | Phase 2–3 | $3M in accelerated aging (Phase 2) | >2% degradation per 1,000 cycles |
| Radiative coating degradation in field | Phase 3 | $2M in coating R&D (Phase 2) | >15% reflectance loss in Year 1 |
| Cultural rejection of panel aesthetics | Phase 3 | $1M in community co-design (Phase 3) | >30% occupant dissatisfaction |
| Building code delays | Phase 4 | $3M in regulatory advocacy (Phase 3–4) | No ICC-ES submission by Year 6 |
